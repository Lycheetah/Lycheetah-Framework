## THE THEOREM

**Theorem (Framework Incompleteness):**

> Any framework F that (a) is expressive enough to model its own coherence state Ψ,
> and (b) honestly reports its own limitations, must contain at least one statement
> that is true within F but unprovable by F.

**Proof sketch:**

Suppose F is a framework that measures its own coherence via Ψ ∈ [0,1].

Define statement G: "This framework cannot prove its own coherence is maximal."

**Case 1:** G is provable within F.
Then F proves it cannot prove Ψ = 1. But proving this statement requires
F to have complete knowledge of its own proof-theoretic limits — which means
F can enumerate all its own theorems. By a diagonalization argument analogous
to Gödel's, this leads to contradiction: F would need to be both the system
and the meta-system simultaneously.

**Case 2:** G is not provable within F.
Then there exists a true statement (G itself, since F genuinely cannot prove Ψ = 1
from finite evidence) that F cannot prove. F is incomplete.

**In both cases, F contains truths it cannot reach.**

∎
