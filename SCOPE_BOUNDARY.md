*This document exists to prevent misattribution. Every item below is a claim the framework explicitly does not make, paired with the closest claim it does make and why the distinction matters. ## 1. EARNED LIGHT does not claim to solve the hard problem of consciousness

**What it does NOT claim:** That the framework explains why there is "something it is like" to be conscious. The hard problem — why physical processes give rise to subjective experience — is explicitly outside scope.

**What it DOES claim:** That consciousness can be modeled as a maintained thermodynamic asymmetry field A(ψ, x, t), with computable density C_ψ(t) = ∫A(ψ,x,t)dx, at thermodynamic cost ΔH_s = −W/T. This is a claim about one measurable, computable property correlated with consciousness — not a claim about subjective experience.

**Why the distinction matters:** Confusing these leads to dismissing the thermodynamic model because it doesn't explain qualia, when it makes no such claim. The model is testable via metabolic correlates. The hard problem is not.

**Source:** `06_EARNED_LIGHT/essentials.md` — "Hard problem of consciousness is outside framework scope" (claim ELI-004, ACTIVE).

## 2. ANAMNESIS does not claim mystical pre-existence of knowledge

**What it does NOT claim:** That mathematical truths exist in a Platonic realm that humans access through mystical recollection; that the convergence of independent traditions proves non-physical knowledge transmission.

**What it DOES claim:** That TC(S, n) — the transcultural convergence metric — measures when n independent traditions converge on structure S from different starting points. That this convergence is better explained by attractor dynamics in epistemic space than by cultural diffusion alone. That mathematical structures (φ, π, symmetry groups, Fibonacci) achieve TC ≥ 3–4.

**Why the distinction matters:** "Anamnesis" is a Greek term for recollection, used here as a precise technical name for the convergence phenomenon — not as a metaphysical claim about pre-existence. The framework explicitly retracted the claim that "Gödel's incompleteness theorems prove Platonism" (claim RET-001). The convergence data is empirical. The Platonic interpretation is not.
