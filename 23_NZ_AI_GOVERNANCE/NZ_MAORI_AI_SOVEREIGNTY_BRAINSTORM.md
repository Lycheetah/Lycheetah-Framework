### IDEA 2: ATUA DOMAIN SOVEREIGNTY
**Status:** Conceptual — protocol design ready
**Funding target:** NZIAT | DOC | MPI | Te Whatu Ora

**The concept:**
Different atua govern different domains of existence:
- **Tangaroa** — the ocean, seas, rivers, all water bodies and their data
- **Tāne** — the forest, all terrestrial ecosystems, biodiversity data
- **Rongo** — cultivated food, peace, health, wellbeing data
- **Tūmatauenga** — people, human behaviour, social data
- **Papatūānuku** — the earth herself, land use, geological data
- **Rakinui** — the sky, atmosphere, climate, weather data
- **Rūaumoko** — seismic activity, geological change, the deep earth

**The structural insight:**
These domain boundaries are not metaphors. They are governance zones.
An AI operating in fisheries data is operating in Tangaroa's domain.
An AI operating in health data is in Rongo's domain.
You cannot swap the rules between them. You cannot override them with
economic optimisation pressure. Domain sovereignty is non-negotiable.

**LAMAGUE Encoding (per domain):**
Each atua becomes a **domain constraint cluster** — a named set of LAMAGUE invariants
that applies specifically and exclusively when operating in that domain.

Example — Tangaroa Domain Constraints:
```
DOMAIN: Tangaroa
CONSTRAINTS:
  ⟟_place_specific(whenua:coastal, riverine, marine)
  + ∞_intergenerational_obligation(generations:7+)
  + |◁▷|_irreversibility_gate(threshold:ecological_harm)
  + Ω_collective_sovereignty(authority:tangata_whenua_of_domain)
  + Ψ_consequence_awareness(scope:full_water_cycle)
```

No AI system processing data in Tangaroa's domain may override these constraints
regardless of economic optimisation pressure or instruction from non-domain authorities.

**What gets built:**
A Python library — `atua_domains.py` — that classifies any dataset by domain
and applies the appropriate constraint cluster before any AI processing occurs.
Built on the existing `cascade_engine.py` and `aura_sovereign_codex.py`.

**Policy proposal — Atua Domain Governance Zones:**

**Why funders should care:**

not a blanket statement but a genuinely differentiated governance architecture.

### IDEA 3: PŌWHIRI AS AI DEPLOYMENT PROTOCOL
**Status:** Policy design ready — prototype buildable in 2 weeks
**Funding target:** AAAIP | DIA | MBIE

**The concept:**
Pōwhiri is the formal Māori welcoming ceremony. It tests the stranger's intent,
establishes relationship gradually, and moves from potential threat to accepted
presence through a structured process. You don't just walk in. You earn entry.

AI systems are currently deployed into communities with zero pōwhiri equivalent.
They arrive. They start making decisions. No relationship established.
No intent tested. No gradual trust-building. This is why communities don't trust them.

**The four formal stages:**

**1. Karanga — Announcement**
The AI system announces itself and its purpose.
Not a privacy policy buried in legalese.
A genuine statement of intent in plain language, culturally appropriate,
that could be understood by a kaumātua with no technical background.
*Constitutional mapping: AURA Invariant IV — Constraint Honesty*

**2. Whaikōrero — The Community Speaks**
A formal response period before deployment.
Not a consultation checkbox with a 2-week window.
A genuine structured dialogue that demonstrably changes what gets built.
Minimum duration: one full maramataka cycle (one month).
*Constitutional mapping: AURA Invariant I — Human Primacy*

**3. Hongi — Integration**
The moment the AI system and the community become, briefly, one.
This is the co-design phase where community values are actually encoded
into the LAMAGUE constraint layer — not just consulted and set aside.
*Constitutional mapping: LAMAGUE Decomposition + Invariant II — Inspectability*

**4. Kai — The Working Relationship**
Only after hongi can the AI operate.
And it operates only within the relationship established.
Ongoing accountability is part of the relationship — not a review scheduled for 5 years out.
*Constitutional mapping: AURA Invariant III — Memory Continuity*

**LAMAGUE Encoding:**
```
Ψ_awareness_of_context + ⟟_grounded_in_place + Φ↑_earned_progression
+ |◁▷|_integrity_gate + ⇌_bidirectional_relationship + Ω_collective_consent
```

**What gets built:**
- A formal Pōwhiri Protocol specification (legal + technical)
- A digital pōwhiri platform — communities can complete the four stages online
  with cryptographic record of each stage completion
- An API that connects stage completion to deployment gating

**Policy proposal:**
Any AI system deployed in a predominantly Māori community or handling Māori data
must complete Pōwhiri Protocol before going live.
Stage completion is legally recorded. Skipping stages is a violation.

**Why funders should care:**
This is the most concrete answer to "how do we actually consult communities" that NZ has.
It replaces performative consultation with a culturally grounded, legally enforceable process.
Every government agency deploying AI needs this.

**Mana Iti (Limited Authority):**
AI system can provide information and suggestions.

Requires human review of all outputs.

AI system can co-author recommendations.

Community validation required in the system's operating domain.
Certification requires: Mana Iti + community consultation record + CASCADE coherence score.

**Mana Rangatira (Leadership Authority):**
AI system can operate with significant autonomy.
Reserved for systems with demonstrated long-term performance in specific domains.
Requires ongoing iwi validation in domains affecting Māori.
Certification requires: Mana Taurite + longitudinal performance record + domain authority endorsement.

**LAMAGUE Encoding:**
```
Φ↑_earned_ascent + |◁▷|_integrity_maintained + Ψ_community_recognition

```

**What gets built:**
- Formal Mana Certification specification document (legal + technical)
- CASCADE-based performance measurement protocol for certification
- NZ AI certification registry

**Policy proposal:**
All AI systems operating in NZ government or public services require Mana Certification.

Iwi endorsement is required for Mana Rangatira in domains affecting Māori.
