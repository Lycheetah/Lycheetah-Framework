## WHY THIS MATTERS

The default assumption in research is:
credibility flows from institution → researcher → work.

The Lycheetah Framework inverts this:
the work either holds or it doesn't.
Reality has a vote.
The institution is irrelevant to that question.

This is not anti-institutional posturing.
It's the only honest position when no institution was involved.

The mathematics in `11_MATHEMATICAL_FOUNDATIONS/` doesn't care
whether it was written at Oxford or in Dunedin.
The proofs either hold or they don't.
The code either runs or it doesn't.
The experiments either replicate or they don't.

That's the credential.

## WHAT WAS BUILT AND WHY

The Lycheetah Framework emerged from a single question:

*What would it mean to build AI that genuinely cannot harm?*

Not "AI with safety guidelines."
Not "AI that tries not to harm."
AI where harm is architecturally impossible —
where the constraints are load-bearing, not aspirational.

The question led to formal ethics (AURA).
Formal ethics led to knowledge architecture (CASCADE).
Knowledge architecture led to a translation protocol (LAMAGUE).
The translation protocol led to tikanga Māori.
Tikanga Māori led back to the original question —
and showed that Māori governance traditions had been asking it
for a thousand years longer than Western AI safety research.

That's not a narrative that was constructed after the fact.
It's the actual sequence. The CLAUDE.md session logs record it.
The GitHub commit history shows it.

## WHAT SELF-TAUGHT MEANS HERE

Self-taught doesn't mean uninformed.

The mathematical foundations in `11_MATHEMATICAL_FOUNDATIONS/`
cover: category theory, differential geometry, dynamical systems,
operator algebra, sheaf theory, information theory, and Kolmogorov complexity.

These were not studied through formal coursework.
They were studied because the framework demanded them —
because each successive layer of the architecture required a more precise tool,
and the tool was found in the mathematical literature and learned.

This is a different relationship to knowledge than credential-based learning.
It's not superior. It produces different blind spots and different insights.

The blind spots: there may be errors in the proofs that a formally trained
mathematician would catch immediately.
This is why scrutiny is explicitly invited.

The insights: the frameworks weren't shaped by a field's
existing assumptions about what questions are worth asking.
They were shaped by the questions themselves.

## WHAT INDEPENDENT MEANS HERE

Independent means: no funder, no supervisor, no institution
shaping what can be said or how it can be said.

This creates a specific kind of freedom.
The LAMAGUE Te Reo layer could be built without:
- worrying about whether it would offend partner institutions
- filtering through an ethics review board designed for a different kind of research
- being shaped by what the grant application required it to say

It also creates a specific kind of risk.
Without institutional backing:
- There's no built-in credibility signal
- There's no team to catch errors
- There's no infrastructure for sustained development

The min-max strategy for this risk:
Make the work so clearly demonstrable that institutional backing becomes
a question of resource rather than legitimacy.
The legitimacy has to come from the work.
The resources can come from institutions once the work is visible.

That's what this folder is for.
