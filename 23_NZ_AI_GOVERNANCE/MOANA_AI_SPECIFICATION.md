## WHAT IT DOES

### Climate Intelligence
- Ocean temperature monitoring — real-time, Pacific-wide
- Sea level tracking with island-specific projections
- Extreme weather prediction calibrated for Pacific geography
- Coral reef health monitoring (CASCADE coherence applied to ecosystem data)
- Fisheries stock modelling with traditional fishing knowledge integration

### Environmental Knowledge
- Integration of mātauranga / traditional ecological knowledge
  alongside scientific data — treated as epistemically equivalent,
  not supplementary
- Taniwha-style relational models: environment-community relationships
  modelled as units, not separated
- Seasonal and navigational knowledge preservation and computational
  cross-referencing

### Governance Support
- Cross-nation policy coherence analysis (CASCADE)
- Resource sharing optimisation across Pacific nations
- Climate adaptation planning with intergenerational modelling
- International negotiation support (unified Pacific data voice)

## GOVERNANCE ARCHITECTURE

### Constitutional Layer (AURA)
All seven AURA invariants apply. The system cannot:
- Be used against any participating nation's interests
- Prioritise any single nation's data over another's
- Operate without transparency to all governance partners
- Make decisions without human override from affected communities
- Extract value without reciprocal return (VTR ≥ 1.0 for each nation)

### Cross-Cultural Layer (LAMAGUE)
Each participating nation's governance tradition is encoded using LAMAGUE.
The encodings are produced BY that nation's governance experts —
not imposed from outside.

Structural convergences between traditions are identified and become
the constitutional foundation of the Moana AI. Divergences are preserved
and respected — the system operates within the intersection of all
traditions, not a single one.

### Decision Layer
No decision is made by the Moana AI. All outputs are advisory.
Decision authority rests with the human governance structures of
each participating nation. The system illuminates; it does not decide.

## BUILD PATHWAY

```
Year 1:  Partnership formation
         - Pacific Island Forum engagement
         - 3 pilot nation agreements (suggest: Cook Islands, Samoa, Tonga)
         - LAMAGUE encoding workshops with each nation's governance experts
         - NZ infrastructure setup (cloud hosting, data governance framework)

Year 2:  MVP — Climate monitoring + traditional knowledge integration
         - Ocean/weather monitoring for 3 pilot nations
         - Traditional ecological knowledge digitisation (community-led)
         - CASCADE coherence engine deployed
         - AURA constitutional framework operational

Year 3+: Scale — Full Pacific deployment
         - Additional nation onboarding
         - Fisheries, agriculture, health modules
```
