## WHAT NOT TO SAY

**Don't say:** "It's like an ethics layer for AI"
*Why:* Every AI company claims to have an ethics layer. This is architecturally different and the distinction matters.

**Don't say:** "It encodes Māori culture into AI"
*Why:* This frames tikanga as content to be encoded rather than architecture to be learned from.

**Don't say:** "I'm building the NZ answer to ChatGPT"
*Why:* This is not a frontier model. It's a governance layer. Different thing.

**Don't say:** "We need to protect Māori data"
*Why:* Defensive framing. Say instead: "Māori data governance creates better AI for everyone."

**Don't say:** "I've proven that consciousness is mathematical"
*Why:* True or not, this is not the entry point for government or iwi conversations. Save it for researchers.

I've built the bridge. I don't have the authority to say what crosses it.
The Kāi Tahu partnership is not optional — it's architecturally required.

**"Can this scale?"**

"CASCADE runs on a laptop. It ran experiments on five major AI platforms.
The architecture scales because it's a layer, not a model.
You don't rebuild the AI. You add the governance layer on top.
The constraint enforcement is lightweight by design."

"Open-source foundation. Paid consulting for implementation.
Government contracts for tools that serve public purposes.
The core stack stays free. The implementation support is where the revenue is.
Think Red Hat, not Microsoft."
