## THE FOUR STANDARDS — WHAT NZ CAN DO NOW

These are implementable. Not proposals requiring years of development.
The architecture is built. The standards need political will and institutional adoption.

### 1. Community AI WOF
**"Has it got its WOF?"**

Annual certification for AI systems in NZ public services.
Seven checks. Pass/fail. Independent assessors. Public register.
Three words every NZ voter understands. No explanation required.

*Full standard: `23_NZ_AI_GOVERNANCE/COMMUNITY_AI_WOF.md`*

### 2. Three Worlds Disclosure
Per-output epistemic labelling grounded in Te Ao Māori:
- **Te Ao Mārama** — what the system knows with high confidence
- **Te Ao Pō** — what the system is uncertain about
- **Te Kore** — what the system structurally cannot know

Applies to every AI output affecting New Zealanders.
*Full standard: `23_NZ_AI_GOVERNANCE/THREE_WORLDS_DISCLOSURE_STANDARD.md`*

### 3. Whakapapa Disclosure
Every AI system must disclose its genealogy at procurement:
- **Tīpuna** — training ancestors (data sources)
- **Hapū** — builders and their obligations
- **Iwi** — who is accountable
- **Mokopuna** — future obligations acknowledged

*Full standard: `23_NZ_AI_GOVERNANCE/WHAKAPAPA_DISCLOSURE_STANDARD.md`*

### 4. Matariki Annual Audit
Accountability as national ritual. Three domains:
- **Whānui** — who was harmed (published)
- **Pōhutukawa** — what was nourished (published)
- **Tupuānuku** — what was received from users (published)

Maximum ten pages. Readable by anyone.
Regulation you can hire your way around. Ritual you are either part of or visibly absent from.

*Full standard: `23_NZ_AI_GOVERNANCE/MATARIKI_AUDIT_STANDARD.md`*
