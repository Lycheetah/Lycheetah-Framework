## THE PROBLEM THIS ADDRESSES

New Zealand faces a structural vulnerability shared by small nations everywhere:

**Monetary dependency** — NZD floats against currencies controlled by economies 100x larger. Financial shocks elsewhere become NZ crises with a 12-hour delay.

**AI dependency** — NZ public services are becoming dependent on AI infrastructure built, owned, and governed by overseas companies. The governance of those systems follows US/EU legal frameworks, not Treaty obligations.

**Infrastructure dependency** — SWIFT, the USD clearing system, and the dominant cloud providers are all vectors through which NZ sovereignty is quietly eroded.

China's response to this is the e-CNY: a state-controlled digital currency that routes around USD clearing infrastructure. The structure is the same authoritarianism with different plumbing.

**The question this document asks:** What would a genuinely different structure look like? Not state-issued fiat in digital form. Something else.

## THE CORE PROPOSAL

**The Aotearoa Sovereign Intelligence Token (ASIT)** — a digital currency whose value is backed not by government decree or commodity reserves, but by **verified AI governance compliance** and **community trust metrics**, audited annually through the Matariki framework.

The backing is not money. The backing is demonstrated trustworthiness.

## THE BACKING MECHANISM [SCAFFOLD]

*The structure here is sound. The specific parameters require economic modelling.*

### Layer 1 — AI Governance Reserve

Every AI system operating in Aotearoa NZ that holds a valid Community AI WOF contributes to the Governance Reserve. The reserve is not money — it is **verified compliance units (VCU)**.

A VCU is issued when:
- A system passes all 7 WOF checks (full VCU)
- A system holds a Conditional WOF with active remediation (partial VCU, 0.5 weight)
- A system achieves Whakapapa Disclosure certification (additional 0.25 weight)

The total VCU pool represents the verified trustworthiness of AI operating in NZ.

ASIT tokens in circulation are backed by the VCU pool. If the VCU pool grows (more systems become genuinely accountable), ASIT gains purchasing power. If systems lose their WOF (accountability failures), VCUs are burned — ASIT's backing contracts.

**The direct mechanism:** accountability has monetary value. Loss of accountability destroys monetary value. The incentive structure is built into the currency.

### Layer 2 — Community Trust Reserve

The second backing layer is the Matariki Annual Audit metrics:
- Whānui score (harm prevented)
- Pōhutukawa score (benefit delivered)
- Tupuānuku score (reciprocity honoured)

These are aggregated into a Community Trust Index (CTI) published annually at Matariki. The CTI acts as a monetary policy anchor — the Reserve (RBNZ-equivalent) adjusts ASIT supply based on CTI direction.

If CTI rises: new ASIT tokens can be issued, distributed to communities that contributed to the improvement.

If CTI falls: ASIT supply is constrained. The economy experiences friction until trust metrics recover.

### Layer 3 — Indigenous Knowledge Sovereignty Endowment [CONJECTURE]

*This layer is the most novel and the most uncertain. It is offered as a direction, not a design.*

Mātauranga Māori — the accumulated knowledge systems of iwi — has economic value that is currently externalised. When overseas AI companies train on New Zealand data (including oral histories, ecological knowledge, cultural practices), they extract value without compensation.

The third backing layer is a **Knowledge Sovereignty Endowment**: a formal accounting of the value of mātauranga Māori embedded in AI systems operating in Aotearoa. Iwi hold endowment certificates. ASIT is partially backed by those certificates.

This makes indigenous knowledge sovereignty a structural feature of the monetary system — not a principle, not an aspiration, but a backing asset.

**Honest caveat:** The valuation methodology for intangible cultural knowledge as a monetary backing asset is unsolved. This layer is [CONJECTURE]. The direction is right. The mechanism requires years of co-design with iwi and economists.

## WHY THIS IS DIFFERENT FROM CRYPTO

Most crypto arguments are about:
- Decentralisation (removing state control)
- Speculation (number go up)
- Technical novelty (blockchain as proof of anything)

ASIT is none of these things.

**ASIT is not decentralised.** It is accountable — to communities, to iwi, to the Matariki cycle. Decentralisation without accountability is not sovereignty. It is abandonment.

**ASIT is not speculative.** Its value is backed by verifiable real-world conditions. The WOF is assessed by humans. The Matariki audit is published in plain language. The backing is readable, not algorithmic.

**ASIT uses distributed ledger technology as an audit trail** — for transparency, not for financial speculation. The ledger's purpose is to make the backing visible: every VCU issued, burned, or transferred is on-record.

The question ASIT asks is not "how do we remove the state from money?" but "what if money reflected what we actually value as a society?"

## THE POLITICAL AND LEGAL PATHWAY [SCAFFOLD]

*This section outlines what would need to happen. It does not pretend this is easy.*

**Step 1 — Reserve Bank NZ engagement**
ASIT is not a replacement for NZD. It is a parallel instrument — closer to a community scrip or a carbon credit than a national currency. Initial engagement would be with RBNZ's policy team, framed as a governance innovation, not monetary competition.

**Step 2 — Iwi partnership on Knowledge Sovereignty Endowment**
Layer 3 cannot be designed without Kāi Tahu and other iwi. The Whakapapa Disclosure work opens this door. Iwi would hold initial governance authority over the endowment certificates.

**Step 3 — Pilot at community scale**
ASIT does not need to launch as a national currency. A pilot within a single region — a hua whenua cooperative, a hapū economy, a community governance initiative — proves the mechanism before scaling.

**Step 4 — WOF register as the reserve backing institution**
Once the Community AI WOF Register exists (see MINISTERIAL_BRIEFING.md), VCU issuance has a formal institutional basis. The register *already does this work* — ASIT just makes its outputs monetarily consequential.

## HONEST FAILURE MODES [NIGREDO]

This proposal has real vulnerabilities. They are named here, not buried.

**Failure mode 1 — Gaming the WOF**
If WOF certification has monetary value, agencies have incentive to pass the WOF by surface compliance rather than genuine accountability. Mitigation: the WOF assessor is required to be independent; the Failure Museum publishes all WOF failures publicly; gaming is detectable.

**Failure mode 2 — CTI manipulation**
If governments can adjust ASIT supply by adjusting CTI methodology, the backing becomes political. Mitigation: CTI methodology is constitutionally fixed; changes require supermajority governance process; iwi hold veto over methodology changes.

**Failure mode 3 — Speculation enters anyway**
Any digital asset with liquid markets attracts speculation regardless of backing design. Mitigation: ASIT is initially non-tradeable on external exchanges; community-to-community transfer only; speculation requires years of governance evolution to enter.

**Failure mode 4 — The knowledge endowment valuation is unresolvable**
There may not be a methodology that valuates mātauranga Māori as a reserve asset without doing harm to the knowledge itself — commodifying what should not be commodified. This is the deepest uncertainty. It is why Layer 3 is [CONJECTURE] and why iwi partnership is not optional.

## THE SIMPLEST VERSION

If all of this is too much:

**Version 0:** Issue ASIT as community tokens, not currency. Every agency that passes its WOF gets tokens. Tokens are publicly visible on a leaderboard. No monetary value. The visibility is the incentive.

**Version 1:** ASIT tokens are exchangeable for government services at a discount — agencies that hold valid WOF certification get reduced procurement fees, faster regulatory approvals, preferred supplier status.

**Version 2:** ASIT becomes a B2G (business-to-government) currency layer — companies dealing with NZ government can hold and spend ASIT as a trust signal, backed by their governance compliance.

**Version 3:** The full monetary instrument described above.

Start at Version 0. Build upward from reality, not downward from theory.

## WHAT MAKES THIS NEW

No country has done this. China has not done this. The EU has not done this.

The EU AI Act issues compliance certificates. They have no monetary consequence.

China's e-CNY routes around USD infrastructure. It does not encode governance accountability into the monetary unit itself.

**What ASIT proposes is that accountability should be economically rewarded and non-accountability should be economically costly — not through regulation and fines, but through the structure of value itself.**

If you govern AI well, your currency is worth more.
If you govern AI badly, your currency loses backing.
The incentive is not external. It is constitutionally embedded.

That is new.
