## PART 1: THE PROPOSAL (500-word version)

### Title
**Formally Structured AI Governance for Aotearoa: LAMAGUE as a Computable Bridge Between Tikanga Māori and Technical AI Standards**

### The Problem
New Zealand's AI governance documents commit to "alignment with NZ values" and "Treaty principles" — but these commitments exist only as aspirations in policy text. No technical mechanism currently converts tikanga concepts into computable constraints that AI systems must satisfy. The gap between "AI aligned with our values" and an actual AI system that demonstrably satisfies those values remains unbridged.

Meanwhile, overseas AI systems are becoming default infrastructure in NZ public services — health, welfare, education, justice — without any mechanism to verify whether those systems align with NZ's constitutional commitments, including Treaty obligations.

### The Proposed Research
The Lycheetah Framework includes LAMAGUE (Language of Alignment, Measurement, and Governance for Universal Ethics) — a formal mathematical protocol that converts governance concepts into computable AI constraints. LAMAGUE has already been used to encode Western ethical frameworks (Kantian categorical imperative, utilitarian value transfer, constitutional AI invariants) as formal operators.

This research proposes to test whether tikanga Māori governance concepts — specifically kaitiakitanga, mana, whakapapa, tapu/noa, and kotahitanga — can be formally encoded using LAMAGUE, and whether AI systems operating under those encodings demonstrably behave differently from systems without them.

**Research questions:**
1. Can tikanga governance concepts be encoded as LAMAGUE formal operators without losing their relational and contextual meaning?
2. Do AI systems constrained by tikanga LAMAGUE encodings produce outputs that iwi assessors rate as more culturally appropriate?
3. Does the formal encoding process surface misunderstandings of tikanga that require iwi correction — and if so, does that correction process produce a more accurate encoding?

**The critical constraint:** LAMAGUE Te Reo encodings are [PROPOSAL] status. They cannot become validated standards without iwi partnership. This research programme is structured around that requirement — Kāi Tahu iwi partnership is an architectural requirement, not an optional component.

### What Already Exists
The Lycheetah Framework is not speculative:
- CASCADE knowledge architecture: Lyapunov stability proven (Theorem 3.1) + convergence chain (3.1→3.3→4.2) ACTIVE for discrete implementation; Python implementation live
- AURA Seven Invariants: formally specified, measurably testable; coherence floor C ≥ 0.70 is a proven mathematical condition (not just a design choice)
- LAMAGUE: operational grammar with Python implementation (`lamague_reference.py`)
- **Four implementable NZ AI accountability standards** (no legislation required):
  Community AI Warrant of Fitness, Three Worlds Disclosure, Whakapapa Disclosure, Matariki Annual Audit
- **The Governance Stack** (`THE_GOVERNANCE_STACK.md`): seven-layer architecture showing how all standards connect mathematically
- **LAMAGUE cross-cultural convergence worked example** (`GOVERNANCE_CONVERGENCE_WORKED.md`): three traditions (tikanga Māori, Confucian, Westminster) formally encoded with four structural convergences identified
- **CASCADE governance application** (`CASCADE_GOVERNANCE_APPLICATION.md`): truth pressure formula applied to policy coherence with worked NZ water policy example
- Thirteen Python implementations across four tiers
- arXiv preprint: CASCADE framework (endorsed, live)
- GitHub: github.com/Lycheetah/Lycheetah-Framework
- 1,402 pages of documented development history
- Public Failure Museum: eleven exhibits of framework failures, nothing removed

The funding is for the cross-cultural validation research — specifically the iwi partnership and empirical testing that converts a well-structured proposal into a validated standard. The standards are already built. The validation is the remaining step.

### Expected Outputs
**Year 1:**
- Kāi Tahu-reviewed LAMAGUE encodings of five core tikanga concepts (corrected, validated, attributed)
- Empirical study: AI outputs rated by iwi assessors under tikanga-constrained vs. unconstrained conditions
- Publication: "LAMAGUE as a cross-cultural AI governance protocol: Māori tikanga case study" (target: AI & Society or AI and Ethics, Springer)

**Year 2:**
- Draft LAMAGUE Te Reo Governance Standard (Kāi Tahu as lead author alongside Mac)
- Pilot deployment: one NZ government agency using tikanga LAMAGUE constraints in a real AI decision
- Policy brief to MBIE, Te Arawhiti, and NZIAT on formal implementation pathway

## PART 5: WHAT MAKES THIS DIFFERENT FROM OTHER MAORI AI PROPOSALS

Most Māori AI governance proposals operate in one of two modes:
- **Policy advocacy** — argues that AI governance should include tikanga, but doesn't specify how
- **Cultural overlay** — adds Māori language or imagery to existing AI systems without changing the underlying constraints

This proposal operates in a third mode:
- **Constitutional architecture** — formally encodes tikanga as the constraints that govern AI behaviour at the mathematical level

The difference is the difference between a policy document that says "AI should respect mana" and an AI system that provably cannot produce an output that violates a formal mana constraint. LAMAGUE enables the second. Nothing else currently does.
