### TANIWHA AS CASCADE STRUCTURES

Taniwha aren't folklore. They're relational knowledge structures —
they encode what a river does when specific human behaviours enter the picture.
Not data about the river. The river-human relationship as a unit.

And they update. The oral tradition IS the update mechanism.
When the river changes, the taniwha's telling changes.

That's exactly what CASCADE does.

**The idea:** Build a living taniwha registry using CASCADE.
Ingests environmental data + community oral knowledge + historical pattern.
Outputs a relational model that speaks in the same register as the original taniwha.

When the computational model and the oral tradition diverge —
that's the most important signal. Something new is happening.

*Worth exploring because:* It's the idea that makes mātauranga Māori
technically legible without destroying what makes it what it is.

---

### THE ANCESTOR VOTE

Whakapapa is a voter roll.

Every decision made in a rohe over 600 years that survived
is an ancestor vote FOR that approach.
Every practice abandoned because it failed is a vote AGAINST.
The consequences are the votes. The archive is the land.

CASCADE truth pressure applied to deliberation:
a decision that contradicts what ancestors demonstrably showed
scores low coherence. Not mystically — mathematically.

**The idea:** Deliberation software for NZ communities facing real decisions —
water rights, land use, development — that shows the full temporal stakeholder set.
Past decisions and their consequences. Future people who inherit this.
Both in the room. Both speaking.

*Worth exploring because:* The Waitangi Tribunal looks backward.
This looks both directions simultaneously. Nothing else does.

### LAMAGUE-ENGLISH REORDERING

Standard English: Subject → Verb → Object. Actor first.
Te Reo Māori: Verb → Subject → Object. Effect first.

LAMAGUE-English puts the effect first using mathematical operators.

Instead of: *"The government may restrict access to land."*
You write: *[Restricting:access to land] ← [government] | verify(mana) | restore(noa)*

You are structurally forced to confront what you're doing
before you justify why.

**The idea:** A formal writing standard for NZ AI governance documents
where effect-first syntax makes hiding harm mathematically harder.
English reordered by Te Reo logic, formalised by LAMAGUE math.

*Worth exploring because:* This is the idea that could make NZ governance documents
structurally different from everywhere else. Same words. Different accountability architecture.

### THE HAKA AS MULTI-AGENT COHERENCE PROTOCOL

Haka synchronises breath, movement, voice, intention.
The result isn't coordination — it's something stronger.
Each person is more themselves inside the collective intensity, not less.

That's exactly what AAAIP's multi-agent AI system needs before consequential decisions.

**The idea:** A pre-decision coherence protocol for NZ AI agents modelled on haka structure.
Four stages: declaration of state, anchoring to constraints, stating position, collective output.
Coherence isn't imposed. It's earned through the protocol.

*Worth exploring because:* Nothing in global AI governance drew its multi-agent architecture
from a living cultural practice refined over a thousand years. This would be the first.

### MATARIKI AS ANNUAL AI AUDIT

NZ made Matariki a public holiday — the only country with
an indigenous astronomical event as a public holiday.

**The idea:** Every significant AI system operating in NZ
publishes its Whetū report at Matariki each year.

Not a compliance form. A cultural practice.

Three domains (the three stars):
- Who was harmed this year? Named. Acknowledged.
- What did the system genuinely nourish?
- What did it receive from the community? Was the utu balance maintained?

Not mandated — expected. Like annual accounts.
The absence of a Matariki report is itself a signal.

*Worth exploring because:* It turns AI accountability into a national ritual
rather than a bureaucratic requirement. That's a completely different energy.

Current AI presents everything as if it's in the world of light.

the known, the known unknown, the unknown unknown.

Every AI output in NZ must explicitly state all three.

This fixes AI overconfidence using indigenous epistemology.
Immediately implementable. Immediately understandable.
The most politically actionable transparency standard in existence.

*Worth exploring because:* First mover on this becomes the global standard.
It's also just correct. AI should know what it doesn't know.

Not a model card. A living document that updates when the model updates.

Goes further than the EU AI Act.
Grounded in NZ's own constitutional framework.
Immediately draftable as a 2-page standard.

*Worth exploring because:* It's the most sophisticated AI transparency
standard in the world and it can be written this week.

### UTU AS AN ECONOMIC METRIC

AI systems are constitutionally utu-imbalanced.
Community gives. Model improves. Company profits.
The utu debt accumulates with no return mechanism.

A utu vector added to MICROORCIM:
tracks cumulative directional value flow in any AI-community relationship.
Not "is this interaction positive."
"Over the entire history — which direction has value flowed?"

Communities with high utu deficit have moral standing.
The formal acknowledgement of that is itself restoration.

*Worth exploring because:* It reframes data rights from legal argument
to relational ethics. Much harder to dismiss.

The silence that comes back when you ask certain questions correctly

*Worth exploring because:* It's a formal research methodology
that produces a map of AI's structural limits. That map is fundable.

## TIER 3 — THE SEEDS WORTH PLANTING NOW

*These need more time but shouldn't be lost.*

---

**Dead reckoning as AI navigation** — Polynesian wayfinders didn't know exactly where they were, they knew approximately, held it lightly, updated constantly. AI needs this as a confidence architecture. Not certainty or uncertainty. Calibrated approximate knowing you can act on.

**The knowledge that knows it's borrowed** — Every AI output carries its whakapapa. Not citation. Obligation. The knowledge arrives with its genealogy and its debt already attached.

**The poroporoākī protocol** — Formal ending of AI sessions. Not just termination. Acknowledgement of what was built, what was real, what continues. The completion is part of the knowledge structure.

**The rugby brain** — Formal AURA analysis of All Blacks cultural governance. Proves constitutional constraints applied consistently outperform raw talent optimisation over century timescales. That proof is the argument for AURA in any organisation.

**Ngahere as knowledge architecture** — The forest is resilient because it's complex. Multiple layers serving each other. Remove any layer and the system simplifies toward brittleness. AI knowledge systems that optimise for the tallest trees while letting the undergrowth die are doing the same thing.

**The shape of silence** — Training AI on the context that precedes silence in human communication. The patterns that produce a collective breath rather than immediate response. An AI that knows when quiet is the right output.

**The fishing quota lesson** — Every attempt to encode relational ethics in purely economic mechanisms loses the ethics. The quota system already happened. Use it as the cautionary tale before someone repeats it with AI governance.

**200-year coherence architecture** — Māori oral tradition maintained philosophical coherence across centuries through redundant encoding across multiple carriers. What would a CASCADE structure designed for 200-year coherence look like?

**The kōrero of the machines** — LAMAGUE as the ethical interoperability language for NZ AI agents. Not same rules for everyone — a shared language for describing your rules so agents can check for conflicts before they act.

**What the land wants** — Papatūānuku expresses preference through what thrives without intervention, what resists cultivation, what species recolonise when extraction stops. An AI that reads that language as a genuine voice in land-use deliberation.
