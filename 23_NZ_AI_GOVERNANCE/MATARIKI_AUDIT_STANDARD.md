## THE STANDARD

Every significant AI system operating in Aotearoa publishes its
**Whetū Report** at Matariki each year.

Not a compliance form. A cultural practice.

Three domains. Named after three of the Matariki stars:

### WHĀNUI — Who Was Harmed?

Named. Not statistical. Named where consent is given. By community where it isn't.

This section answers:
- Who was harmed by this system in the past 12 months?
- What was the nature of the harm?
- Was it systemic (design failure) or incidental (edge case)?
- What was done about it?
- What remains unresolved?

**The requirement:** Harm is acknowledged publicly. Not as legal admission —
as relational responsibility. The people affected are recognised as people,
not data points.

### PŌHUTUKAWA — What Did This System Genuinely Nourish?

Specific. Attributed. Verified.

Not marketing claims. Not aggregate satisfaction scores. Specific instances
of genuine benefit to specific communities or individuals (with consent).

This section answers:
- What did people receive from this system that they wouldn't have otherwise?
- Can the benefit be independently verified?
- Is the benefit proportionate to what the system took?

**The requirement:** Benefits are claimed only when they are real and verified.
"Our platform served 2 million users" is a usage statistic, not a benefit claim.
A benefit is: "this system identified a misdiagnosis for [person/community]
that resulted in [specific positive outcome]."

### TUPUĀNUKU — What Did This System Receive From the Community?

The utu accounting.

This section answers:
- What data was taken? From whom? With what consent?
- What attention was given? How much human time did this system consume?
- What labour was extracted? Did users generate value that the system captured?
- What is the cumulative utu balance? Which direction has value flowed?
- What is the system's plan to restore balance?

**The requirement:** The debt is acknowledged. The reciprocity is declared.
If the utu balance runs negative — if the system has taken more than it
returned — that is stated plainly and a restoration plan is named.

## FORMAT

The Whetū Report is a public document. Maximum 10 pages. Readable by
anyone — not technical, not legal, not corporate.

```
[SYSTEM NAME] — WHETŪ REPORT [YEAR]
Published: Matariki [date]

1. WHĀNUI — Who Was Harmed (2-3 pages)
   - Named harms
   - Systemic vs incidental
   - Actions taken
   - Unresolved matters

2. PŌHUTUKAWA — What Was Nourished (2-3 pages)
   - Verified benefits
   - Specific instances
   - Proportionality assessment

3. TUPUĀNUKU — What Was Received (2-3 pages)
   - Data taken and consent status
   - Value flow direction
   - Utu balance
   - Restoration plan (if negative)

4. COMMITMENT — For the Year Ahead (1 page)
   - What will change
   - What will be measured
   - How the community can hold us accountable
```

## WHY RITUAL, NOT REGULATION

Regulation you can hire your way around.
Ritual you are either part of or visibly absent from.

The Matariki audit is not mandated. It is **expected.** Like annual accounts.
Like the WOF. Like a birth announcement.

The systems that publish Matariki reports become trustworthy.
The systems that don't — their silence is itself the signal.

Compliance is transactional. Ritual is relational.
The difference is the difference between a contract and a promise.

NZ made Matariki a public holiday because the stars deserve recognition.
AI systems that affect people's lives deserve the same level of public
accounting — once a year, honestly, in a form everyone can read.
