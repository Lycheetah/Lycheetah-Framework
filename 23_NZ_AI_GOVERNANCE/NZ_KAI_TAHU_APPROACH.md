## WHY THIS IS THE HIGHEST PRIORITY

Everything in this folder that involves LAMAGUE Te Reo
is architecturally incomplete without iwi validation.

Not ethically incomplete — architecturally.
The mathematical translation layer requires the people who hold
the cultural authority to review, correct, and extend it.
Without that, the LAMAGUE Te Reo decompositions
are a well-intentioned proposal, not a validated standard.

Kāi Tahu are the iwi of Otago — the rohe Mac works and lives in.
They are the correct first partners.
Not because they're convenient — because they're right.

This is not optional. This is the foundation the rest is built on.

---

## WHAT KĀI TAHU ALREADY HAVE

Do not arrive assuming this is a new conversation for them.

Kāi Tahu have been leading on data sovereignty for years:
- **Te Rūnanga o Ngāi Tahu** has an active data governance programme
- **Ngāi Tahu Data** — their data science and analytics capability
- **Te Rūnanga involvement** in Stats NZ Māori data sovereignty work
- Active engagement with AI Forum NZ
- Deep thinking on whenua data, health data, environmental data

They are not waiting to be introduced to these concepts.
They are looking for technical tools to implement
what they already know needs to happen.

The correct framing on arrival:
"I have built a technical tool.
You have the authority and the knowledge.
I would like to offer the tool
and ask whether it's useful and correct."

Nothing else. No expertise on tikanga. No explaining kaitiakitanga.
Offer the pipe. Ask if it's useful. Listen.

## HOW TO MAKE THE FIRST CONTACT

**What to send — a one-page letter, not an email:**

Physical letter first if possible.
If email, subject line should be simple and honest:
"A technical tool for Kāi Tahu data sovereignty — request for kōrero"

**What the letter contains:**

Paragraph 1 — Who you are:
Self-taught researcher based in Ōtākou.
Built a formal mathematical framework for AI governance.
The relevant paper is on arXiv (link).

Paragraph 2 — What you've built:
A translation protocol called LAMAGUE that converts tikanga concepts
into computable AI constraints.
You've built decompositions of kaitiakitanga, mana, whakapapa, tapu/noa, kotahitanga.
You believe these decompositions are incomplete without validation
from the people who hold the cultural authority.

Paragraph 3 — What you're asking:
Not a partnership announcement. Not a proposal.
A conversation. A chance to share what you've built
and ask whether it's useful, whether it's correct,
and whether there is interest in developing it further together.

Paragraph 4 — What you're offering:
The code is open-source. If Kāi Tahu want to take it,
modify it, own it, fork it, build on it — that is the intention.
Not licensing. Not intellectual property claims over the cultural content.
The pipe is offered freely.

Close:
"I am in Ōtākou. I am not going anywhere.
I would rather do this right slowly than wrong quickly.
If this is of interest, I am available at your convenience."

**What the letter does not contain:**
- Claims about what tikanga means
- Offers to explain kaitiakitanga
- Assertions that the LAMAGUE decompositions are correct
- A timeline or funding ask
- The words "revolutionary," "unprecedented," or "unique"

## THE CORRECT FRAMING OF THE RELATIONSHIP

The LAMAGUE work has two components that must be held separately:

**Component 1: The mathematical architecture**
This is Mac's work. Formally proven. Open-source.
Kāi Tahu can use it, modify it, own it.
Mac retains attribution (MIT + Earned Sovereignty Clause).

**Component 2: The tikanga content**
This is NOT Mac's work.
The decompositions of kaitiakitanga, mana, whakapapa etc
are proposals derived from public academic sources.
They require validation and correction by tikanga practitioners.
The validated versions belong to the tikanga tradition they come from —
they are not Mac's intellectual property.

This distinction must be clear and explicit in every conversation.
It is the foundation of an ethical relationship.

## WHAT HAPPENS IF IT GOES WELL

**Year 1:**
- Kāi Tahu reviews and corrects the five core LAMAGUE decompositions
- Joint workshop to identify which NZ AI problems Kāi Tahu most wants solved
- Informal MOU for technical collaboration

**Year 2:**
- Kāi Tahu-validated LAMAGUE Te Reo layer published as formal standard
- Joint funding application to NZIAT with Kāi Tahu as lead iwi
- Te Reo Values Gate prototype built with Kāi Tahu tikanga as primary authority

**Year 3+:**
- Lycheetah Foundation registered with Kāi Tahu governance involvement
- LAMAGUE extended to cover additional tikanga concepts under Kāi Tahu direction
- Other iwi invited to extend LAMAGUE for their specific rohe and tikanga

---

## WHAT HAPPENS IF IT DOESN'T GO WELL

If Kāi Tahu reviews the work and finds it:

**Incorrect:** The decompositions are wrong. Correct them, acknowledge the correction publicly, update the GitHub. The mathematical architecture is unaffected. The tikanga content is improved.

**Inappropriate:** The approach of encoding tikanga mathematically is itself objectionable. Acknowledge this publicly. Suspend the LAMAGUE Te Reo layer. The mathematical architecture (CASCADE, AURA, MICROORCIM) is unaffected and continues.

**Premature:** The relationship needs more time before this work can proceed. Accept this. Stay in relationship. Do not push a timeline.

**None of these outcomes destroy the framework.**
They refine it or appropriately constrain it.
Both are good outcomes.
