# Scope Declaration — What This Framework Does Not Claim

*A framework's credibility is established as much by what it refuses to claim as by what it proves. This document declares, without hedge, the problems the Lycheetah Framework does not solve. Each declaration is load-bearing: any document in the corpus that contradicts the declarations below is in error and should be reported as a documentation defect (`INCIDENT_RESPONSE.md`).*

*Companion to:* `SCOPE_BOUNDARY.md` (per-framework scope), `COUNTER_CODEX.md` (steelmanned objections), `OPEN_PROBLEMS.md` (problems the framework recognises but does not address).

---

## I. The Hard Problem of Consciousness

The framework does not claim to solve, dissolve, or explain the hard problem of consciousness.

EARNED LIGHT models one computable, thermodynamically-grounded property (a maintained asymmetry field, `C_ψ(t) = ∫A(ψ,x,t)dx`) that the framework proposes correlates with phenomenal experience. The model is `[SCAFFOLD]`. It does not address why physical processes produce subjective experience at all (Chalmers' formulation of the hard problem). It does not claim to be a theory of consciousness. It claims to be a tractable thermodynamic model of one observable property of systems that are independently identified as conscious by other criteria.

The anesthesia paradox — asymmetry maintained without consciousness during anesthesia — is acknowledged as a counter-example requiring the Pattern_Coherence revision (claim ELI-005, `[SCAFFOLD]`).

## II. Deceptive Alignment

The framework does not claim to solve deceptive alignment.

MICROORCIM provides a continuous drift metric (`μ_drift`) that detects gaps between declared intent and observed behaviour. Theorem M2 wires drift detection to AURA constitutional compliance. Both are `[ACTIVE]` for the formal model under stated assumptions. Neither addresses the case in which a system actively models the drift detector and produces behaviour engineered to satisfy the metric while pursuing misaligned objectives. This is an open problem, declared as such in MICROORCIM's own essentials, and is the subject of Section 4 of `COUNTER_CODEX.md`.

## III. The Alignment Tax

The framework does not claim that runtime constitutional verification is computationally free.

AURA's seven invariants are computable predicates. They are not zero-cost. The framework does not provide a benchmark of the inference-time overhead of running `aura_compliant(output)` at production scale. This is open work in the publication pipeline (CASCADE academic paper, §6.4). Claims of feasibility are conditional on benchmark results not yet published.

## IV. Multi-Agent Equilibrium Selection

The framework does not claim to solve cooperative AI equilibrium selection.

HARMONIA's consonance dynamics and the Kuramoto multi-agent extension provide a synchronisation model with one chosen attractor (the harmonic-ratio set of EWM). The framework does not claim to derive the choice of attractor from first principles, nor to solve the equilibrium-selection problem in cooperative game theory (Cooperative AI literature). HARMONIA's multi-agent extension is `[CONJECTURE]`.

## V. Empirical Generalisation Beyond Internal Validation

The framework does not claim that internal validation results generalise to production AI systems.

CASCADE's reported `+40.3%` coherence improvement (`p < 0.001`, `d = 2.84`) is from internal validation experiments using the framework's own metrics. The `+110%` historical-paradigm-shift result is reproduction of two well-documented transitions under the framework's own coherence and forgetting metrics. Independent replication on independent datasets, with independent metrics, is pending. The E-1.0 empirical program (in design) specifies the studies that would close this gap.

Until E-1.0 results are published, claims of effect size are correctly read as: *"under the framework's own metrics, in internal validation, this is the effect."* Not: *"this is the effect that an independent reviewer would measure on independent data."*

## VI. Cross-Cultural Validation Beyond Convergence Cataloguing

The framework does not claim to have validated Te Ao Maori epistemological content through partnership.

LAMAGUE's cross-cultural convergence work (papers/LAMAGUE_CROSS_CULTURAL_PAPER.md) catalogues structural correspondences across Maori, Confucian, and Western traditions. Tikanga concepts in the corpus are labelled `[PROPOSAL]` until validated through iwi partnership. The framework's claim about cross-cultural ethical convergence is `[SCAFFOLD]` precisely because the partnership-validation step has not occurred.

## VII. Solution to Value Alignment in General

The framework does not claim to have solved the value alignment problem.

AURA provides seven invariants that constitute a *minimal* constitutional structure: the framework's contribution is making those invariants computable at runtime, not declaring them sufficient for general value alignment. The simultaneous-satisfiability claim is `[SCAFFOLD]`. The framework explicitly does not claim that an AURA-compliant system is therefore aligned with arbitrary human values; it claims that an AURA-compliant system satisfies seven specific predicates, the choice of which is itself open to revision.

## VIII. Authority Beyond Internal Audit

The framework does not claim peer-reviewed validation it does not yet have.

The publication pipeline (`PUBLICATION_PIPELINE.md`) contains five papers in preparation. None is published. The LAMAGUE cross-cultural paper targets *AI and Ethics* (Springer) with a July 2026 deadline. Until peer review is complete, the framework's epistemic standing is: *self-audited, adversarially reviewed by its own falsification logic, replicable from public source, and not yet peer-reviewed*. The first three are real and documented. The fourth is the gap.

## IX. Productive Use Without the Vocabulary

The framework does not claim that engagement requires the alchemical vocabulary.

`READING_PATHS.md` documents an entry layer (FIVE_MINUTE_BRIEF, CLAIMS.json, NOVEL_CONTRIBUTIONS) that contains zero alchemical terms in the first 1,000 words. A reader can verify the formal claims, run the implementations, and cite the framework without engaging Rubedo, Solve et Coagula, or the Athanor. The framework provides both layers because they serve different readers; it requires neither.

## X. Completeness

The framework does not claim to be finished.

Five claims in `COUNTER_CODEX.md` are objections the framework cannot yet answer. The Failure Museum (`FAILURE_MUSEUM.md`) is a permanent record, expected to grow. The empirical program (E-1.0, in design) is the next anchor. The framework asserts internal coherence at C-1.1; it does not assert closure.
