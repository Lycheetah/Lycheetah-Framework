Four AI Accountability Standards

Ideas and proposals generated using the Lycheetah Framework for NZ AI governance. These stack — each operates at a different timescale. Together they form a complete accountability architecture no other country currently has.

Community AI WOF

"Has it got its WOF?" Annual certification for AI systems in NZ public services. Seven checks. Independent assessors. Public register. Pass/fail.

Three words every NZ voter understands. No explanation required.

Read the standard
Three Worlds Disclosure

Per-output transparency grounded in Māori epistemology.

Te Ao Mārama — what the system knows.
Te Ao Pō — what is uncertain.
Te Kore — what cannot be known.

Every AI output labelled by its epistemic status.

Read the standard
Whakapapa Disclosure

Every AI system should be able to tell you its genealogy.

Four layers: Tīpuna (training ancestors) · Hapū (builders) · Iwi (accountable) · Mokopuna (future obligations).

Where it came from. Who it is. What it owes.

Matariki Annual Audit

Annual accountability as national ritual. Three domains named after Matariki stars: Whānui (who was harmed) · Pōhutukawa (what was nourished) · Tupuānuku (what was received). Maximum 10 pages. Readable by anyone.

Regulation you can hire your way around. Ritual you are either part of or visibly absent from.
