## II. DOMAIN PARAMETERS

### E = Psychological Evidence Strength
Not just logical evidence. Lived experience.
- Trauma that contradicts safe-world belief
- Success that contradicts "I'm incapable" belief
- Loss that contradicts "things last forever" belief
- Measurement: Emotional intensity × frequency × salience

### P = Prior Violation (Surprise)
How much does this evidence violate what was believed?
- Small P: Fits existing worldview (barely registers)
- Medium P: Challenges some beliefs (disturbing but manageable)
- Large P: Shatters core worldview (existential shock)
- Measurement: Distance from core identity beliefs

### S = Psychological Coherence (Identity Cost)
How integrated is the person's belief system?
- High S: Rigid identity ("I am X, always will be")
- Medium S: Flexible identity (multiple modes, context-dependent)
- Low S: Fragmented identity (inconsistent beliefs, dissociation)
- Measurement: How much would changing one belief force change in others?

### Critical Π Threshold
Personal level: Π ≈ 1.2 (humans resist change less than organizations, more than languages)
- Below 1.2: Cognitive dissonance, rationalization, denial
- Above 1.2: Reorganization starts (therapy, crisis, breakthrough)
- Way above (Π > 2.0): Shattering reorganization (trauma, radical conversion)

## III. OPERATIONAL WORKFLOW

### Input: Personal Narrative
Person describes:
- What they believed before
- What happened (the evidence)
- How it changed them
- What they believe now

### Calculate Personal Π

Example: Trauma-informed belief reorganization

```
Before: "The world is safe. People are trustworthy."
Event: Betrayal/assault/loss (high emotional intensity)

E = Emotional evidence strength: 0.9 (vivid, ongoing)
P = Prior violation: 0.95 (shatters "safe world" completely)
S = Identity coherence: 0.5 (person had flexible identity)

Π = (0.9 × 0.95) / 0.5 = 1.71  ← CRITICAL

Below 1.2: Denial/rationalization ("It wasn't that bad")
At 1.2-1.5: Cognitive dissonance (nightmares, hypervigilance, questioning)
Above 1.5: Reorganization (new beliefs emerge, identity shifts)
At 1.71: Full reorganization

After: "The world is dangerous. Trust must be earned."
New cascade: Integration phase (therapy, healing, sense-making)
```

### Trajectory Mapping
Track how belief system reorganizes:
1. **Resistance phase** (Π < 1.2): Denial, rationalization
2. **Dissonance phase** (Π 1.2-1.5): Contradiction visible, disturbing
3. **Reorganization phase** (Π > 1.5): Old beliefs collapse, new ones form
4. **Integration phase** (post-reorganization): New identity consolidates

### Healing Prediction
Given a person's Π and S, predict:
- Will they reorganize, resist, or fragment?
- How long will integration take?
- What support accelerates healing?
- What could destabilize them?

## IV. CASCADE SEQUENCES IN PSYCHOLOGY

### Pattern 1: Developmental Reorganization
Childhood → Adolescence → Adulthood (natural cascades)

Example: Adolescence identity reorganization
- E: Peer pressure, body changes, social exclusion (high)
- P: Adult behavior expectations violate "kid rules" (high)
- S: Child identity was coherent; becomes inadequate
- Π ≈ 1.8 (normal adolescent crisis)
- Result: Identity reorganizes (sometimes smoothly, sometimes catastrophically)

### Pattern 2: Trauma Reorganization
Single intense event → Identity shatters → Reorganizes

Example: Sudden loss (death, breakup, job loss)
- E: Loss event (maximum emotional intensity, E = 1.0)
- P: "This shouldn't happen to me" violated (P = 1.0)
- S: Identity included this person/role (S = 0.3)
- Π = (1.0 × 1.0) / 0.3 = 3.3 (EXTREME)
- Result: Identity dissolution + reorganization (grief → acceptance → integration)

### Pattern 3: Slow Accumulation
Small contradictions over years → Sudden reorganization

Example: Burnout reorganization
- Years of: overwork (E slow-builds), "I'm capable" challenged (P increases), identity = work (S high)
- After years: Π reaches 2.1
- Result: Sudden collapse ("I can't do this anymore")
- Reorganization: "My worth isn't my productivity"

### Pattern 4: Healing Reorganization
Therapy works by intentionally raising Π safely
- Therapist provides evidence of reality (E increases)
- Challenges limiting beliefs (P increases)
- In safe relationship, S stays manageable
- Π rises to reorganization threshold
- New beliefs form, integrated by therapist support

## V. PSYCHOLOGY CASCADE METRICS

### Belief System Coherence (BSC)
```
BSC = (identity_integration × narrative_consistency × behavior_alignment) / cognitive_load

High BSC: Rigid, resistant to change, fragile under pressure
Low BSC: Flexible, open to change, resilient
Optimal: Medium BSC (coherent but adaptable)
```

### Reorganization Readiness Score (RRS)
```
RRS = Π + (therapy_support × 0.5) - (trauma_overwhelm × 0.3)

Predicts whether person will reorganize or fragment.
RRS > 0: Reorganization likely
RRS < -1: Fragmentation likely (dissociation, denial)
```

### Integration Velocity (IV)
```
IV = emotional_processing_rate × social_support × meaning_making_ability

Predicts how fast someone integrates new beliefs after reorganization.
Faster IV = faster healing.
```
