## WHAT WE NEED FROM A CHINESE PARTNER

The ideal Chinese research institution brings:

1. **Confucian philosophy expertise** — scholars who can validate
   the LAMAGUE encodings of Chinese governance concepts as accurate
   and culturally legitimate

2. **AI safety / AI ethics research capacity** — ability to co-run
   the empirical AI system testing in Chinese contexts

3. **English-language research output** — for joint publication

4. **MBIE relationship or credibility** — some prior NZ engagement helps
   but is not required for Catalyst applications

5. **Independence** — ability to do honest research, not just validate
   preferred conclusions

## TIER 1 — MOST ALIGNED

### 清华大学 Tsinghua University — Beijing

**Why they fit:**
- Institute for AI International Governance (人工智能国际治理研究院)
  is China's leading AI governance research centre
- Published work on cross-cultural AI ethics and global AI governance
- Connected to China's government AI policy (New Generation AI Governance Principles)
- Strong international partnerships — used to working with overseas researchers
- Computer Science + Philosophy intersection already present

**Key contact points:**
- Institute for AI International Governance (AIIG)
- Centre for AI Safety at Tsinghua
- Department of Philosophy (has Confucian ethics focus)

**How to approach:**
Via MBIE's international science team or via Asia New Zealand Foundation.
A warm introduction through NZ's MBIE or through an NZ academic
already connected to Tsinghua is ideal.

**Research fit:**
Tsinghua AIIG is actively working on AI governance frameworks
that are not purely Western. A NZ-Tsinghua collaboration that
provides formal mathematical foundations for Chinese governance
concepts in AI would be directly aligned with their stated research agenda.

**Key contact points:**

- School of Advanced Interdisciplinary Studies

## SENSITIVITY NOTE

Research with Chinese institutions requires awareness of:

1. **Topic sensitivity:** AI governance is a politically sensitive topic in China.
   The research is academic and formal, not politically critical. It validates
   Chinese governance philosophy rather than critiquing it. This is the correct
   framing and it is accurate.

2. **Data sovereignty:** Any data collected on Chinese participants
   in the empirical testing phase must comply with both NZ Privacy Act
   and Chinese data governance requirements.

3. **Publication independence:** Any research agreement must preserve
   the right of both parties to publish findings independently,
   including findings that complicate the convergence hypothesis.
   AURA Invariant VI (Non-Deception) applies to the research itself.

4. **Cultural authority:** The Confucian encodings require validation
   by Chinese scholars who hold cultural authority — this is the same
   principle as the Kāi Tahu requirement for the Māori encodings.
   The framework is consistent: cultural content requires cultural authority.
