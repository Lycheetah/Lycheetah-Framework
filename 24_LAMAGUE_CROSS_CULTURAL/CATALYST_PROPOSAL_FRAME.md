## THE OPPORTUNITY IN ONE PARAGRAPH

The Catalyst Strategic NZ-China fund is looking for joint research partnerships
in Artificial Intelligence. China has a sophisticated AI governance tradition
drawing on Confucian ethics and state governance philosophy.
New Zealand has the Lycheetah Framework — a formally developed constitutional AI
architecture that includes LAMAGUE, a mathematical translation protocol
that converts governance concepts into computable AI constraints.
Both countries have demonstrated commitment to AI governance that goes beyond
Western liberal defaults. The research question: do Confucian, Māori, and Western
governance traditions encode structurally identical AI safety constraints —
and can LAMAGUE serve as the cross-cultural bridge that makes this testable?
The answer matters to every country trying to build AI governance that is
both formally rigorous and culturally legitimate.

## PART 1: THE PROPOSAL IN FULL

### Title
**Constitutional AI Governance Across Cultures: LAMAGUE as a Cross-Cultural
Bridge Between Māori, Confucian, and Western Governance Traditions**

### Research Question
Can a formal governance language (LAMAGUE) identify structurally convergent
AI safety constraints across independently developed philosophical traditions —
specifically Māori tikanga, Confucian philosophy, and Western formal ethics —
and can AI systems governed by these convergent constraints demonstrate
measurably higher cross-cultural governance coherence?

### Why This Matters

**For New Zealand:**
- Positions NZ as a global leader in AI governance that is both technically rigorous
  and culturally grounded
- Provides the mathematical foundation for the NZ Digital Strategy's aspiration
  to be "aligned with NZ values" — making that aspiration technically implementable
- Advances Treaty obligations by demonstrating that tikanga-based governance
  is formally equivalent to, not merely alongside, other legitimate governance traditions
- Builds the NZ-China research relationship in a domain where NZ has genuine expertise

**For China:**
- Provides formal mathematical tools for expressing Confucian governance philosophy
  as computable AI constraints — something Chinese AI safety research needs
- Offers an internationally credible governance framework that is not
  purely Western liberal in its foundations
- Produces research relevant to China's stated AI governance objectives
  (New Generation AI Governance Principles 2019)
- Creates a pathway for Chinese AI systems to demonstrate governance legitimacy
  in Pacific and Māori contexts

**For the world:**
- The first formally developed cross-cultural AI governance framework
- A response to the governance legitimacy crisis in AI: whose values should AI follow?
- Empirical evidence that some governance constraints are universal, not merely Western

### Research Objectives

1. **Formal convergence analysis:** Produce LAMAGUE encodings of core Confucian
   governance concepts through collaboration with Chinese philosophy scholars.
   Test structural isomorphism with existing Māori and Western LAMAGUE encodings.

2. **Empirical validation:** Apply CASCADE truth pressure to governance claim sets
   from all three traditions. Test whether the convergent constraints emerge
   as higher-Π content than tradition-specific elaborations.

3. **AI system testing:** Build AI system configurations governed by:
   (a) Single-tradition LAMAGUE constraints
   (b) Cross-cultural convergent LAMAGUE constraints
   Test cross-cultural governance coherence ratings from representative user groups
   in both NZ and China.

4. **Standard development:** Draft a LAMAGUE Cross-Cultural Governance Standard
   that could serve as the basis for a bilateral NZ-China AI governance protocol
   and potentially for regional Pacific-Asia adoption.

### Methodology

**Year 1 — Formal Analysis**
- LAMAGUE encoding workshops with Chinese philosophy scholars
  (target: Tsinghua University or Peking University Department of Philosophy)
- Structural isomorphism analysis — compare NZ-derived and China-derived encodings
- CASCADE analysis of all three tradition's governance claim sets
- Draft convergence map (formalising CONVERGENCE_MAP.md)

**Year 2 — Empirical Testing**
- Build AI agent configurations using cascade_engine.py + aura_sovereign_codex.py
- Cross-cultural governance coherence testing in NZ and Chinese contexts
- User evaluation panels: NZ Māori participants + Chinese participants
  rating governance appropriateness of AI outputs under different constraint sets
- Statistical analysis of convergence vs. divergence

**Year 3 — Standard Development and Publication**
- Draft LAMAGUE Cross-Cultural Governance Standard
- Submit to AI Forum NZ, AAAI, Pacific Forum for adoption consideration
- arXiv paper: "Constitutional AI Governance Across Cultures: Empirical Evidence
  for Cross-Traditional Convergence" (joint NZ-China authorship)
- Policy brief for MBIE, NZIAT, and Chinese counterpart agencies

## PART 5: WHAT THIS IS NOT

**This is not a translation project.**
We are not building a Mandarin translation of the Lycheetah Framework.
We are testing whether Confucian governance philosophy independently encoded
the same structural constraints as tikanga Māori and Western ethics —
and whether those convergent constraints can serve as the foundation
for AI governance that is legitimate across all three traditions.

**This is not political.**
The research is formal and empirical. It produces testable predictions
and falsifiable hypotheses. It does not require political alignment
between NZ and China on anything beyond shared interest in
AI governance that actually works.

**This is not about Chinese AI systems specifically.**
The research produces a cross-cultural governance standard applicable
to any AI system operating in contexts where multiple governance traditions
have legitimate claims. That includes both NZ (Māori/Western) and
any Pacific-Asian context (Confucian/Western/indigenous).
