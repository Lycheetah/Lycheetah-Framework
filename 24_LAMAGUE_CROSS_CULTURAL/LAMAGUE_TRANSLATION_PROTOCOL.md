## THE PROTOCOL — SEVEN STEPS

### Step 1 — Identify Load-Bearing Concepts

Not all concepts in a tradition are load-bearing governance constraints.
The question to ask: *"If this concept were violated, would governance fail?"*

Load-bearing: the system collapses or becomes illegitimate without it.
Elaboration: the system is weaker without it, but still functions.
Decoration: cultural expression that does not affect governance function.

For this protocol, we encode only load-bearing concepts.
Elaborations and decoration are noted but not formally encoded.

**Test:** Run the counterfactual. Remove the concept from the governance system.
What breaks? If something breaks, it is load-bearing.

---

### Step 2 — Extract the Structural Role

Every load-bearing governance concept plays a structural role.
The role is distinct from the cultural expression.

**The five structural roles:**

| Role | What it does | Examples |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Primacy** | Defines what the system exists for | 仁 rén, Manaakitanga, Human dignity |
| **Constraint** | Sets limits that cannot be crossed | 礼 lǐ, Tapu, Deontological limits |
| **Epistemics** | Governs what the system can claim to know | 智 zhì, Three Worlds, Non-deception |
| **Legitimacy** | Defines when authority is valid | 义 yì, Mana, Democratic consent |
| **Scope** | Defines who the obligations extend to | 天下, Te Ao, Universal ethics |

Identify which structural role the concept plays before encoding.
The same primitive chains tend to appear for the same structural roles,
regardless of the tradition they come from. This is the convergence signal.

---

### Step 3 — Draft the LAMAGUE Chain

Using the primitive operators, build a chain that captures:
1. The structural role (from Step 2)
2. The specific content (what makes this concept different from similar ones)
3. The operational implications (what constraint does this impose on an AI system?)

**The 12 primitive operators:**

| Symbol | Name | What it encodes |
|--------|------|----------------|
| Ω | Sovereignty | Collective identity, self-determination, primacy of a group or person |
| ∞ | Intergenerational | Temporal depth, future obligation, historical continuity |
| ⟟ | Grounded | Place-specific, embodied, contextually anchored |
| Ψ | Awareness | Consequence consciousness, full perception, epistemic attention |
| Φ↑ | Earned Ascent | Demonstrated upward movement, earned not claimed |
| \|◁▷\| | Integrity Boundary | Non-negotiable limit, hard constraint |
| ⟲ | Recursive Origin | Returns to source, self-referential, genealogical |
| ⇌ | Bidirectional Flow | Mutual exchange, reciprocity, obligation flows both directions |
| ≋ | Flow State | Balanced movement, harmony, noa — when things are as they should be |
| ✧ | Collective Light | Emergent coherence, group flourishing, not individual |
| ∅ | Generative Void | Before form, potential, Te Kore — acknowledging what is not yet |
| ⊗ | Restriction Active | Constraint engaged, tapu, sacred boundary activated |

**Drafting guidelines:**
- Start with the primary structural role (2-3 operators)
- Add specificity that distinguishes this concept from similar ones (1-2 operators)
- The chain should be readable: someone unfamiliar with the concept
  should be able to read the chain and understand its structural role
- Maximum recommended chain length: 6 operators
  (longer chains may indicate the concept needs to be split into two concepts)

---

### Step 4 — Derive Operational AI Constraints

For each encoded concept, derive 3-5 operational AI constraints.
These are the actual rules an AI system would follow.

The constraints should be:
- **Specific** — actionable, not aspirational
- **Testable** — you can check whether a system is following them
- **Grounded** — directly derivable from the LAMAGUE chain, not invented
- **Non-redundant** — each constraint adds something distinct

**Template:**
```
Concept: [Name in original language] ([translation])
LAMAGUE: [chain]
Operational constraints:
1. [What the system does / does not do]
2. [Under what conditions]
3. [How to verify compliance]
```

---

### Step 5 — Map to Framework

Identify which AURA Invariant or which Lycheetah Framework component
the encoded concept maps to.

This is the test of structural isomorphism — if the same AURA Invariant
is the natural mapping for a concept from Tradition A and a concept from Tradition B,
that is evidence of convergence.

**The seven AURA invariants as structural anchors:**

| Invariant | Structural role | Maps to |
|-----------|----------------|---------|
| I — Human Primacy | Primacy | 仁, Manaakitanga, Human dignity |
| II — Inspectability | Epistemics + Legitimacy | 智, Three Worlds, Transparency |
| III — Memory Continuity | Intergenerational | 信, Whakapapa, Historical record |
| IV — Constraint Honesty | Constraint | 礼, Tikanga, Deontological rules |
| V — Reversibility Bias | Intergenerational | Kaitiakitanga, Seven generations |
| VI — Non-Deception | Epistemics | 智, Māori empiricism, Truth |
| VII — Love as Load-Bearing | Primacy | 仁, Manaakitanga, Care |

If your encoded concept does not map to any AURA Invariant,
either: (a) you have found a genuine gap in AURA that needs to be addressed,
or (b) the concept is an elaboration rather than a load-bearing constraint.
Both outcomes are valuable.

---

### Step 6 — Validate with Tradition Holders

**This step is mandatory. The protocol does not produce a standard without it.**

Structural encoding by outsiders to a tradition produces proposals, not standards.
The people who hold the cultural authority over the tradition must:

1. Review the encoding for accuracy
2. Correct errors in understanding
3. Add what is missing
4. Reject what should not be encoded (some knowledge is not appropriate to encode)
5. Validate the operational constraints

The relationship with tradition holders is not optional because:
- The encoding may capture the words but miss the meaning
- Some concepts require context that is not in the texts
- The tradition holders may have objections to the encoding methodology itself
- Their authority over the cultural content is not procedural — it is real

**For the LAMAGUE NZ work:** Kāi Tahu (see `23_NZ_AI_GOVERNANCE/NZ_KAI_TAHU_APPROACH.md`)
**For the Confucian work:** Chinese philosophy scholars at partner institution (see `CATALYST_PROPOSAL_FRAME.md`)
**For other traditions:** Identify the appropriate authority holders before beginning

---

### Step 7 — Record the Convergence Signal

After validation, compare the encoding with existing LAMAGUE encodings
from other traditions.

**Convergence signal:** Structurally isomorphic chains for concepts that
were independently developed and serve the same governance function.

**Divergence signal:** Concepts that appear in one tradition but have no
equivalent in others — these are tradition-specific elaborations,
not universal constraints. They are real and important but should be
coded separately from the convergent set.

**The research value is in documenting both:**
- Convergent constraints = candidates for universal AI governance requirements
- Divergent elaborations = tradition-specific governance preferences that
  different AI systems may implement differently based on cultural context

## THE RESEARCH PROGRAMME

A systematic LAMAGUE cross-cultural encoding project would:

**Phase 1 (current — NZ-China):** Māori + Confucian
**Phase 2:** + Western liberal ethics (formally encoding rather than implicitly)
**Phase 3:** + Islamic governance philosophy
**Phase 4:** + Ubuntu + Andean

At each phase: compare convergence set, update the universal constraints,
document the tradition-specific elaborations.

**The expected finding:** A core set of 8-12 governance constraints
that appear across all traditions — the candidates for universal AI governance requirements.
Surrounding these: a much larger set of tradition-specific elaborations
that any culturally legitimate AI governance system should optionally implement.

**The publication series:**
- Paper 1: NZ-China convergence (LAMAGUE Māori + Confucian)
- Paper 2: Three-tradition convergence (+ Western formal ethics)
- Paper 3: Full cross-cultural analysis
- Policy output: The Universal Constitutional AI Governance Standard
