## THE PROBLEM THIS ADDRESSES

Every major AI system in 2026 is built on an English-language epistemic architecture.
When it operates in te reo Māori contexts, it translates. When it operates in Mandarin
contexts, it translates. Translation means:

1. The original meaning passes through an English-language filter
2. That filter carries embedded assumptions about knowledge, relationship, time, and obligation
3. What arrives in the target language has been partially converted to fit those assumptions
4. What cannot be converted is either dropped or distorted

**Specific examples of what gets lost in translation:**

In te reo Māori:
- *Kaitiakitanga* — translated as "stewardship" but losing the relational, continuous,
  ancestral obligation dimension
- *Whakapapa* — translated as "genealogy" but losing the living-present, obligation-generating,
  cosmological dimension
- *Mauri* — often translated as "life force" but losing its specific role as the
  animating principle of a relational ecosystem
- *Mana* — translated as "prestige" or "authority" but losing the earned, relational,
  transferable, and fragile quality that makes it a governance concept not a status concept

In Mandarin:
- *仁 rén* — translated as "benevolence" but losing the specific relational structure
  where the virtue exists between people, not in an individual
- *義 yì* — translated as "righteousness" but losing the context-sensitivity that
  makes it a situational judgment, not a rule
- *禮 lǐ* — translated as "ritual propriety" but losing the constitutional role of
  structured relationship as the substance of governance, not its form
- *面子 miànzi* — "face" loses the specific governance mechanism by which relational
  accountability operates through social visibility

**The consequence for AI governance:**

An AI system governing Māori communities through an English-language epistemic
architecture is not operating in te ao Māori — it is operating in a translation
of te ao Māori that systematically loses the concepts most important for
genuinely tikanga-aligned governance.

Te Pūkōrero proposes a different architecture.

## THE ARCHITECTURE

### Three Registers, Not Three Languages

Te Pūkōrero is not a translation system. It is a **register-holding system**.

Each of the three traditions — te ao Māori, Chinese philosophical tradition,
Western analytical tradition — is held as a complete epistemic register:
its own structures for knowing, its own governance concepts, its own
relationship between individual and collective, its own temporal framework.

The system reasons within each register in its own terms.
When outputs cross registers, the system marks the crossing explicitly
and preserves what cannot cross.

```
REGISTER 1: Te Ao Māori
├── Epistemic foundation: whakapapa, mauri, tikanga
├── Governance concepts: kaitiakitanga, mana, utu, rāhui
├── Temporal structure: cyclical, intergenerational, ancestral presence
├── Relational architecture: hapū, iwi, wharenui, marae
└── Uncertainty framework: Te Kore (structural unknowability, not ignorance)

REGISTER 2: 中文哲学传统 (Chinese Philosophical Tradition)
├── Epistemic foundation: 道 dào, 理 lǐ (principle/pattern), 气 qì
├── Governance concepts: 仁义礼智信 (the Five Virtues), 中庸 (the Middle Way)
├── Temporal structure: cyclical dynasties, generational continuity, ancestor veneration
├── Relational architecture: 家 jiā (family as unit), 君臣父子 (relational roles)
└── Uncertainty framework: 易 yì (the concept of continuous change as foundation)

REGISTER 3: Western Analytical Tradition
├── Epistemic foundation: empiricism, logic, falsifiability
├── Governance concepts: rights, rule of law, procedural fairness, consent
├── Temporal structure: linear, progressive, institutional
├── Relational architecture: individual → institution → state
└── Uncertainty framework: probability, confidence intervals, error bars
```

## THE LAMAGUE BRIDGE

LAMAGUE is the formal grammar that makes all three registers mutually legible
without collapsing them into each other.

The key LAMAGUE mechanism is the **register-marker**: every governance obligation
encoded in LAMAGUE carries an explicit marker showing which epistemic register
produced it and which registers it is portable to.

```
LAMAGUE primitive notation (simplified):

ψ_māori{kaitiakitanga} ← obligation generated in te ao Māori register
ψ_chinese{仁 rén}       ← obligation generated in Chinese tradition register
ψ_western{accountability} ← obligation generated in Western analytical register

CONVERGENCE: when ψ_māori{X} ≅ ψ_chinese{Y} ≅ ψ_western{Z}
  the convergence is marked as: Ψ_cross{X, Y, Z}
  and recorded in the convergence evidence base

NON-PORTABILITY: when ψ_māori{mauri} has no equivalent in other registers
  the marker is: ψ_māori{mauri}[NOT-PORTABLE]
  and the concept is held in its original register, not translated
```

The NOT-PORTABLE marker is the most important innovation.
It prevents the system from filling a translation gap with a false equivalent.
"We have no equivalent for this" is more honest than a bad translation.

## THE PŪKŌRERO INTERFACE PROTOCOLS

### Protocol 1 — Register Identification

When a user (human or AI) initiates interaction with Te Pūkōrero, the system
identifies the epistemic register of the query and responds within that register.

A query asking about *kaitiakitanga* receives a response in te ao Māori epistemic terms.
A query asking about *仁 rén* in governance receives a response in Chinese philosophical terms.
A query asking about fiduciary duty receives a response in Western legal/ethical terms.

If the query crosses registers explicitly ("how does kaitiakitanga compare to fiduciary duty?"),
the system:
1. Responds within each register first
2. Then identifies structural convergences using LAMAGUE
3. Then explicitly marks what does not converge

### Protocol 2 — Epistemic Marking

All outputs carry register markers:

```
[Te Ao Māori] Kaitiakitanga requires ongoing relationship — not periodic compliance
[Chinese Tradition] 仁 rén is between people, not within one person — it cannot be held alone
[Western Analytical] Accountability is triggered by harm events — it is not continuous
[CONVERGENCE] All three traditions require that those with power account to those without
[NON-PORTABLE: Te Ao Māori] Mauri — the animating relational principle — has no equivalent
```

### Protocol 3 — Community Validation

Te Pūkōrero encodings of te ao Māori are [PROPOSAL] until validated by
Kāi Tahu and other appropriate iwi. Te Pūkōrero encodings of Chinese tradition
are [PROPOSAL] until validated by Chinese philosophical scholars and partners.
Western analytical encodings are [ACTIVE].

No encoding passes from [PROPOSAL] to [ACTIVE] without community validation.
This is non-negotiable.

## THE AI SOVEREIGNTY CLAIM

Te Pūkōrero makes a specific claim about AI sovereignty:

**An AI system that can only reason within one epistemic tradition is not
a sovereign intelligence for Aotearoa New Zealand.**

Aotearoa exists in constitutional relationship with Māori through te Tiriti
o Waitangi. Any AI system making governance decisions in Aotearoa that cannot
reason within te ao Māori — genuinely, not through translation — is not
aligned with that constitutional relationship.

Similarly: any AI system claiming to facilitate NZ-China understanding that
can only approach Chinese philosophical tradition through Western translation
is not actually facilitating understanding. It is facilitating a Western
interpretation of Chinese tradition.

Te Pūkōrero proposes the architectural alternative: three genuine registers,
held simultaneously, bridged by LAMAGUE, with all crossings marked explicitly.

## WHAT DOES NOT YET EXIST

The architecture is specified. What is missing:

1. **Te reo Māori computational linguist** — to validate and extend the
   te ao Māori LAMAGUE encodings with fluency that the framework author
   does not have

2. **Chinese philosophical tradition scholar** — to validate the Confucian
   LAMAGUE encodings and extend them into Daoist and Buddhist governance
   traditions that are also operative in Chinese governance culture

3. **Bilingual training data** — te reo Māori / English governance texts
   that preserve register rather than collapsing to English-dominant framing

4. **The NOT-PORTABLE catalogue** — a systematic documentation of concepts
   that should not be translated, maintained collaboratively by all three
   traditions' representatives

## THE RESEARCH QUESTION

**Can a single AI system hold three genuinely distinct epistemic registers
without one becoming dominant?**

This is not a solved problem. The risk is always that the training architecture
creates a gravitational pull toward one tradition — almost certainly English-language
Western analytical given current training data distributions.

Te Pūkōrero is a proposal for what the solution would look like.
Whether the solution is achievable given current AI architectures —
whether register-holding is possible without register-collapse —
is an open empirical question that someone should research.

The falsification test: does Te Pūkōrero produce outputs that a fluent
te reo Māori speaker judges to be genuinely reasoning within te ao Māori,
not translating from English?

If yes: the architecture works.
If no: the architecture needs revision.

We want to know.
