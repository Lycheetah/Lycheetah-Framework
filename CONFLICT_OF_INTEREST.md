**Purpose:** explicit, pre-emptive disclosure of every interest, relationship, position, or condition that could reasonably be construed as a conflict of interest with respect to the framework, its publications, or its public claims. ## 1. Authorship and originator

The framework (the Codex / Lycheetah / Sol Aureum Azoth Veritas architecture) is the original work of **Mackenzie Conor James Clark**, a self-taught researcher based in Ōtepoti / Dunedin, Aotearoa New Zealand. The author has no formal academic affiliation, no institutional appointment, and no advanced research degree. The work is conducted independently, outside any university or laboratory.

This is disclosed because:
- Self-taught, unaffiliated researchers face systematic epistemic discounting in peer review and grant evaluation. Disclosing the status pre-empts treating the absence of affiliation as a hidden negative signal.
- The work's intended audience explicitly includes academic peer reviewers; the absence of a credentialing institution is structurally relevant to how the work is read.

The author claims no credentialed expertise. The work stands or falls on the evidence and reasoning presented, evaluated by reviewers who have those credentials.

## 2. Life-work relationship

The framework represents a sustained, multi-year program of independent research. The author considers it the central work of his life. This is disclosed because:

- A researcher whose self-conception is bound to a research program has motivated reasoning to defend the program. The framework's defense layer (`ADVERSARIAL_AUDIT_REPORT.md`, `FALSIFICATION_REGISTER.md`, `EVIDENCE_LADDER.md`, this document) is engineered specifically to counteract that motivated reasoning by making claim status falsifiable and demotion procedurally enforced.
- The author acknowledges that this engineering does not eliminate motivated reasoning; it makes it visible and bounded. Reviewers should weight this disclosure when assessing whether claims have been adequately stress-tested.

The framework's `RETRACTED` count (3 of 60 claims as of D-1.1) and the `FAILURE_MUSEUM.md` index of superseded versions are the empirical evidence that the demotion machinery actually fires, not just exists on paper.

## 3. AI tooling and collaboration

The framework's development has involved substantial use of AI systems, primarily Anthropic's Claude family (Opus 4.x, Sonnet 4.x). The disclosure separates roles:

| Role | Performed by | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Original conceptual architecture | Mackenzie Conor James Clark (human) | The core ideas, frameworks, terminology, and theoretical claims originate with the human author. |
| Drafting assistance | Claude (Opus, Sonnet) | Used as a writing collaborator in the same sense as a human editor or co-author would be. |
| Formal verification (proof-checking, internal consistency audits) | Claude (Opus, when run in NRM) + human review | Output reviewed by author before inclusion. |
| Adversarial audit (`ADVERSARIAL_AUDIT_REPORT.md`) | Claude (Opus, in NRM) | Performed under the explicit instruction to find every weakness; results published verbatim. |
| Code implementation (validators, benchmarks, scoring tools) | Claude (Sonnet) under human direction | All code is human-reviewed; testable; published in the live repositories. |
| Substantive intellectual claims | Mackenzie Conor James Clark | The author is responsible for every claim made under the framework's name. |

This is disclosed because:
- Reviewer 2 patterns increasingly include "this work was AI-generated and is therefore not credible" as a dismissal pattern. The disclosure pre-empts that pattern by being explicit about which contributions are which.
- The author makes no claim that AI involvement guarantees the work's correctness. The author also makes no apology for the involvement. Tools are tools; the question is whether the output stands.
- The framework is, in part, a case study in what human-AI collaborative research can produce. This dual role (subject and method) is itself disclosed.

The author's position is that AI use in research drafting is no different in principle from the use of statistical software, proof assistants, literature databases, or human editorial assistance — and should be disclosed in the same spirit, neither hidden nor inflated.

## 5. Geographic and cultural context

The framework is developed in Ōtepoti / Dunedin, on the lands of Kāi Tahu (Ngāi Tahu), in Aotearoa New Zealand. The author acknowledges:

- The work draws on the indigenous epistemologies of multiple traditions (notably in LAMAGUE's cross-cultural convergence work). Where Māori, Kāi Tahu, or other indigenous concepts appear, the author has made best effort to engage respectfully, cite community sources, and avoid extractive use. The author is open to correction and revision where this engagement falls short.
- The author is of European descent (Pākehā). This is relevant where the framework makes cross-cultural claims; the author is not a member of most of the cultural traditions whose convergent patterns the framework documents.
- The author has not yet conducted formal community consultation with Kāi Tahu, Te Tumu (University of Otago Māori, Pacific and Indigenous Studies), or other relevant communities. This is in the work plan; until it is done, claims about indigenous knowledge are framed as observation of public scholarly sources rather than community-validated representation.

This is disclosed because:
- Cross-cultural research carries specific obligations under the Treaty of Waitangi framework in Aotearoa New Zealand and under broader research ethics norms internationally. Pre-emptive disclosure invites scrutiny rather than waiting to be challenged.
- The framework's strongest empirical claim (LAMAGUE cross-cultural convergence) is precisely the area where this matters most, and is held to the higher standard accordingly.

- The author does **not** claim AI co-development guarantees correctness. See § 3.
- The author does **not** claim community endorsement from indigenous communities whose epistemologies are referenced. See § 5.
