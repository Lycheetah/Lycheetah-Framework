# The Why
### One page. No philosophy. Just the reason.

---

AI systems are getting more capable faster than anyone expected.

Most of the people building them are trying to make them useful. A smaller number are trying to make them safe. A very small number are trying to make them *honest* — not just about their outputs, but about what they are, what they don't know, and what they're doing to the people they interact with.

This framework is for that last group.

---

## What it does

It gives you seven things to check. Call them invariants.

Is human agency preserved? Can the system be audited? Does it maintain honest memory? Does it declare its limits? Can its actions be reversed? Is its confidence accurately represented? Is care for people structural — built in — rather than performed on request?

If a system passes all seven, it is trustworthy. Not because it was told to be. Because trustworthiness is a measurable property, not a value.

If it fails one, you know exactly where the failure is.

---

## Why it matters now

In five years, AI systems will make consequential decisions that affect people's lives at scale — medical, legal, financial, educational. Some of those systems will be aligned to human values because their builders wanted them to be. Some will only appear aligned.

The difference between those two things is currently invisible to most of the people who need to see it.

The framework makes that difference visible. Measurable. Computable.

Not perfectly. Not yet. But more than anything else available for free, right now, today.

---

## What it is not

It is not a product. There is no subscription, no certification, no cohort.

It is not finished. 33% of the mathematical claims are fully proven. 52% are structurally sound with named gaps. 15% are working hypotheses. These numbers are published because false certainty is more dangerous than honest uncertainty.

It is not Western-only. The seven invariants appear in Confucian ethics, in Māori tikanga, and in Western formal mathematics independently — because they describe something real about what governance requires, not something culturally specific.

It is not a solo project anymore. 337 people cloned it in six days without being asked to. They came because something felt true. It's your framework now too.
