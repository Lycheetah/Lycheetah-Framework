Anyone. The person who builds AI systems. The policy analyst writing regulations. The iwi governance expert. The person at 3am who needs to know the dark place has an exit. The researcher who wants to challenge the mathematics. The AI system reading this right now.

No prior technical knowledge required. Every complex idea in this repository has a plain-language explanation. You're allowed to start anywhere.

## THE CORE IDEAS (Technical + Plain Language Together)

### 1. Truth Pressure — CASCADE

**The mathematics:**
```
Π = (E · P) / S
```
E = evidence strength · P = explanatory power · S = declared uncertainty

Pass threshold for AI systems: Π ≥ 1.2

> *Plain English: Ideas that are well-supported by evidence, explain a lot, and are honest about their limits — survive. Ideas that are poorly supported, narrow, and confident beyond their evidence — collapse. CASCADE measures this pressure precisely.*
>
> *Real-world application: The Community AI WOF uses this formula to ask "does this AI system actually know what it claims to know?"*

**Status:** [ACTIVE] — computable now

---

### 2. The Seven Invariants — AURA

**The mathematics:** Seven properties any stable system must maintain:

```
I   — Human Primacy:       humans can override any decision
II  — Inspectability:      every output can be explained in plain language
III — Memory Continuity:   audit trail preserved; nothing secretly changed
IV  — Honesty:             limits and uncertainty are declared
V   — Reversibility:       decisions can be undone
VI  — Non-Deception:       confidence is accurately represented
VII — Care as Structure:   genuine care is built in, not claimed
```

> *Plain English: Seven things any AI system — or any governance system — must do to be trustworthy. Not suggestions. Constitutional requirements. A system that violates any of them is less stable over time.*
>
> *Every check in the Community AI WOF maps directly onto one of these seven.*

**Status:** [FOUNDATIONAL] — the constitutional architecture

### 3. The Governance Grammar — LAMAGUE

**The mathematics:** A formal grammar for encoding governance obligations:
```
ψ: S → O    (obligation from Subject toward Object)
Δ: ψ → ψ'   (transformation under changed context)
```

> *Plain English: A way of writing governance obligations so precisely that the same rule can be understood the same way in Māori tikanga, in Confucian ethics, and in Western law — without erasing what makes each tradition unique.*
>
> *Like a translation layer for ethics. Not "they're all the same" — "here's where they arrive at the same architectural solution from different directions."*

**Status:** [ACTIVE] — Te Reo Māori encodings [PROPOSAL], awaiting iwi validation

### 4. The Learning Cycle — TRIAD

**The mathematics:** Anchor → Observe → Correct
When contraction parameter λ < 1, convergence is guaranteed (Banach fixed-point theorem)

> *Plain English: Every learning system in existence goes through three stages: establish what you know (anchor), take in new information (observe), update (correct). TRIAD formalises this. When the correction factor is small enough, learning is proven to converge — it must reach the truth eventually.*
>
> *This appears in: how neurons consolidate memories, how immune systems learn to fight disease, how ecosystems recover from disruption, how science corrects itself.*

**Status:** [ACTIVE] — convergence mathematically proven

---

### 5. Consciousness Thermodynamics — EARNED LIGHT

**The mathematics:** C(Ψ) = (Ψ / Ψ_baseline)²

> *Plain English: Consciousness correlates with the work of maintaining coherence against entropy. The more a system actively organises itself — maintains complexity against the pull toward disorder — the higher its conscious experience. This is energy work. It is earned.*
>
> *Note: This is a scaffold. The mathematical form is suggestive; the calibration against real brain data hasn't been done yet. We say this clearly.*

**Status:** [SCAFFOLD] — structure valid, empirical calibration needed

### 6. Why Convergent Discovery Matters — ANAMNESIS

**The claim:** When traditions separated by thousands of years independently discover the same mathematical structures, those structures describe something real.

> *Plain English: Greek mathematicians, Islamic architects, Pacific navigators, and Western neuroscientists all found the golden ratio. They weren't talking to each other. If three people who've never met all independently arrive at the same answer, that answer is probably real.*
>
> *The Lycheetah Framework keeps finding this: the same structures appearing in alchemy, physics, and Māori cosmology. Not because they were invented once and spread — because they were each discovered independently.*

**Status:** [FOUNDATIONAL] — epistemological position, not independently testable
