The module operates Mandarin-primary discipline (per T-10 [`MANDARIN_PRIMARY_REGISTRY.md`](MANDARIN_PRIMARY_REGISTRY.md)): hanzi appear before Latinised names; classical sources are cited in Chinese before English; engagement with contemporary Chinese AI governance reads original Mandarin documents as primary text where the framework can. Where the framework cannot read the source directly, that limitation is named.

The module is the framework's commitment to engaging Chinese statecraft, governance philosophy, and contemporary AI governance discourse not as area-studies decoration but as primary intellectual partnership.

The operators are not independent — they form a layered architecture:

```

                                  │
   Wuwei (T-4) ────────  Shi (T-3) ────────  Hexie (T-2) ──── Tianxia (T-1)
   correction layer      output–context          within-output          between-agents
   grain-alignment       propensity field        complementarity         flourishing-coupling
   γ(I, S)               σ(O, C)                 H_kl                   Φ_T
                                  │
                                  ↓
                  CASCADE / AURA / TRIAD / HARMONIA
                       (existing framework operations)
```

A framework, deployment, or architectural choice is *fully TIANXIA-coherent* iff all five operators are satisfied. A failure in any layer — ungoverned coupling, collapsed complementarity, context-blind scoring, forced correction, or Datong-degrading direction — is a failure of the whole.

## 与中国当代人工智能治理的接触 — Engagement with Contemporary Chinese AI Governance

The module reads contemporary Chinese AI governance documents as primary texts, not commentary:

- **New Generation AI Development Plan (2017)** — national scale and trajectory; framework's CASCADE governance models multi-agent state-coordinated systems
- **Beijing AI Principles (2019)** — operational requirements (harmony, ethics, accountability, safety) mapped formally to AURA components in T-8
- **Shanghai Declaration on Global AI Governance (2023)** — international cooperation principles; TIANXIA is the framework's response in kind
- **Global AI Governance Initiative (2023, October)** — Xi's affirmative governance proposal; engaged in T-9 as primary text
- **Generative AI Services Regulations (2023)** — first operational regulatory regime for generative AI; framework's implementation work reads against these specifically

The framework does not posit a *Beijing Effect* or evaluate Chinese AI governance against Brussels-Effect or California-Effect benchmarks. It reads each governance artefact as a position in its own right whose internal coherence and external implications are the relevant subjects of analysis.

## 本模块不主张的内容 — What This Module Refuses to Claim

Per Discipline 4 (Negative-Space as Load-Bearing) — see foundation document for full eight declarations. Headline:

1. **No claim of Chinese state authorisation.** This is the framework author's intellectual engagement with primary and secondary Chinese sources.
2. **No claim of cultural authority over the tradition.** The author is a non-Chinese reader; the mapping is structural, not interpretive in the senses reserved for scholars working from within.
3. **No orientalisation.** Every operator has a definition, a primary source, and a formal mapping. If it cannot survive that discipline, it does not enter the module.
4. **No equivalence between Confucian, Daoist, and Legalist sources.** Each tradition is named honestly: Confucian (Hexie via *Analects*, Datong via *Liji*), Daoist (Wuwei via *Daodejing*, *Zhuangzi*), military-strategic (Shi via Sun Tzu), Zhou-dynasty governance recovered contemporarily (Tianxia via Zhao Tingyang).
