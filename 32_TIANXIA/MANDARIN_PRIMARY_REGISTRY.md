## I. What This Registry Is

The TIANXIA module's claim to *primary-source engagement with the Chinese sovereign tradition and contemporary Chinese AI governance* is hollow if the framework's engagement is mediated entirely through English translation. Translation is a research question, not a pre-condition. Direct reading of original Mandarin texts — by the framework's author, with appropriate translation discipline — is the test of the claim.

This registry catalogues:

1. **Primary AI governance documents** — what has been read in direct Mandarin (the *direct-reading log*) and what remains in translation-only engagement (the *pending log*).
2. **Foundational classical sources** — the *Analects*, *Daodejing*, *Liji*, *Sunzi Bingfa*, *Zhuangzi*, and others — and the framework's reading status against each.
3. **Contemporary Chinese AI scholarship** — academic engagements with AI ethics, governance, and philosophy by scholars working from within the tradition.
4. **Open-source model artefacts** — model releases, technical reports, and infrastructure published by Chinese AI teams that constitute primary technical text in the framework's reading.

The registry is honest about current state. As of initialisation (2026-05-01), most primary AI governance documents are catalogued in the pending log, not the direct-reading log. The registry is a commitment to convert pending entries to direct-reading entries over time, with each conversion dated and the engagement implications recorded.

## VII. Translation Discipline

When a direct-reading entry is added to the registry, the following discipline applies:

1. **Original-language passage retained.** The Chinese text of the engaged passage is logged alongside the framework's translation.
2. **Translation rationale recorded.** Where the framework's translation differs from existing standard translations, the rationale is recorded — what the framework reads in the Chinese that the standard translation softens or distorts.
3. **Translator identity declared.** The framework's translation is the framework author's, with explicit acknowledgment that the author is non-native and reads with limitations. Where collaborators translate, attribution is direct.
4. **Reviewer cycle.** Translation entries should ideally be reviewed by a native or near-native reader of Mandarin who is also competent in the relevant tradition (classical for foundational sources; contemporary for governance and scholarship). The framework names this reviewer cycle as a goal; current entries are pending such review.

The discipline is the integrity of the engagement. Without it, the registry is an aspirational catalogue.

---

## VIII. What This Registry Refuses to Claim

1. **Does not claim that the registry is complete.** It is a working catalogue, expected to grow. Absence from the registry is not a judgement on a document's importance.
2. **Does not claim that direct-reading is sufficient for engagement.** Reading is necessary but not sufficient; competent interpretation requires sustained immersion. The framework records what is read, not what is mastered.
3. **Does not claim that the framework's translations are authoritative.** They are working translations for the engagement at hand. Where existing standard translations are widely accepted, the framework defers unless rationale exists.
4. **Does not claim equivalence between classical and contemporary engagement.** Reading the *Analects* in Chinese is structurally different from reading a 2023 governance white paper in Chinese. Both are committed to; the engagement modes differ.
5. **Does not claim that the open-source model contributions in §II constitute the totality of Chinese AI substance.** The list is representative as of registry initialisation; closed-source contributions, infrastructure work, datasets, and benchmarks all extend Chinese AI substance beyond the open-weight catalogue.
6. **Does not claim engagement readiness for all entries simultaneously.** Engagement proceeds at the rate the framework can sustain with discipline. A slow, deep registry is preferred to a fast, shallow one.
