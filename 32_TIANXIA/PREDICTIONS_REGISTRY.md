## I. What This Registry Is

The Position Paper (`POSITION_PAPER_v0.1.md`) names five flagship predictions. This registry extends the framework's falsifiable public stakes to twelve, organised by time horizon, with each prediction operationalised explicitly:

- the prediction itself, stated in falsifiable form
- the time horizon for falsification
- the metric or observable that determines the outcome
- the falsifier — what specifically would force the framework to record the prediction as failed
- the framework component(s) that would downgrade if the prediction fails

The registry is a public commitment device. Each prediction is dated. The framework commits to abide by the falsifiers — no excuse-construction, no goalpost-shifting after the fact, no quiet revision. Failed predictions are recorded permanently in `28_DEFENSE/FAILURE_MUSEUM.md` upon falsification.

Prediction-failures are not framework-failures. A framework that makes twelve predictions and falsifies four of them is more honest than one that makes none. The point is the discipline, not the win-rate.

## III. Aggregation — How Many Predictions Must Hold?

The framework does not predict that all twelve predictions will be confirmed. Honest forecasting expects some failures.

The framework's *aggregate* prediction:

- **Of P-001 through P-004 (short-horizon)**, at least 3 of 4 confirmed by 2027-05-01.
- **Of P-005 through P-008 (medium-horizon)**, at least 3 of 4 confirmed by 2029-05-01.
- **Of P-009 through P-012 (long-horizon)**, at least 2 of 4 confirmed by 2031-05-01.

If the aggregate prediction (3+3+2 = 8 of 12) is met, the framework regards its position as solidified — not because every prediction held but because the directional content of the framework's position survived contact with reality.

If the aggregate is not met, the framework treats the position as having fallen — even if individual predictions held — because the directional content failed.

This aggregation rule is itself a public commitment. It is logged here, dated, and will be honoured at the prediction-review milestones.

## IV. Review Cadence

Each prediction is reviewed at its time-horizon endpoint. Review involves:

1. **Public review post.** A repository commit with the prediction's outcome, dated, with the metric value documented.
2. **Failure Museum entry.** If failed, an entry in `28_DEFENSE/FAILURE_MUSEUM.md` with the failure date, the cause, and the framework component downgrade.
3. **Component status update.** If the component-downgrade rule fires, the affected operator deliverable's status tag is updated in the deliverable's §X (Status, Promotion Path, Downgrade Trigger) section.
4. **No goalpost-shifting.** The framework explicitly commits to not revising the falsifier after the fact. If a prediction was ambiguously specified, the ambiguity itself is recorded as a discipline-failure for future predictions.

The cadence:

- **2027-05-01** — Short-horizon review (P-001 through P-004)
- **2029-05-01** — Medium-horizon review (P-005 through P-008)
- **2031-05-01** — Long-horizon review (P-009 through P-012) and aggregation review

## V. What This Registry Refuses to Claim

1. **Does not claim the predictions exhaust what the framework can be wrong about.** Twelve predictions are not all-the-bets; they are the load-bearing public stakes. Other claims in the framework remain testable on their own terms.
2. **Does not claim the time horizons are sacred.** If a prediction's metric becomes available earlier or later than the stated horizon, the prediction is reviewed at that point with full transparency.
3. **Does not claim the falsifiers are the only valid falsifiers.** Falsification can come through routes the framework did not anticipate. The stated falsifiers are sufficient triggers; other valid falsifiers may emerge.
4. **Does not claim the framework will not learn from failures.** Predictions that fail surface where the framework was wrong; the framework expects to revise. Updating in response to failure is the discipline; it is not goalpost-shifting.
5. **Does not claim that confirmation establishes truth.** A prediction holding does not prove the framework's position; it provides evidence consistent with the position. Multiple confirmations across diverse tests strengthen the position; no single confirmation closes the question.
6. **Does not claim political or strategic prediction expertise.** Some predictions touch geopolitical and strategic dynamics where the framework's expertise is limited. The predictions are made because the framework's positions imply them; the framework's reading of the geopolitical context may itself be wrong, which is one route to the predictions failing for reasons internal to the framework's structural commitments.
