## I. Why Ren Zheng (仁政)

TIANXIA v0.2 forged the five classical operators (Tianxia, Hexie, Shi, Wuwei, Datong). All five are computable; all five reproduce their load-bearing propositions. What is missing:

1. **The benevolence principle** — what distinguishes Wang Dao (王道, kingly way) from Ba Dao (霸道, hegemonic way) — i.e., what distinguishes legitimate governance from coercion that wears governance clothing. This is the load-bearing distinction in Chinese political philosophy from Mengzi forward, and it has no formal counterpart in Western alignment literature.

2. **The constraint side** — Confucian (Tianxia, Hexie, Datong) and Daoist (Wuwei) traditions are present; the Legalist (Han Fei, 韩非) constraint tradition is not. The classical synthesis requires all three.

3. **The change-dynamics ground** — CASCADE belief revision currently grounds in Bayesian formalism. The I Ching (易经) provides the indigenous Chinese epistemology of change; engaging it grounds CASCADE in the classical tradition rather than only Western statistics.

4. **Contemporary resonance without naming** — Datong-aligned long-cycle distributional gradients, Tianxia-aligned multilateral coupling, Hexie-aligned five-fold development composites. The contemporary forms are visible; only the names are not.

5. **AI deployment criteria** — when may AI systems be deployed under Tianxia governance? What does Ren Zheng require of an AI system? This is the practical bridge from theory to policy offering.

Ren Zheng — Mengzi 1B.3, 4A.9 — is the organising operator. *"He who governs through benevolence will become the king of the world; he who governs through force will exhaust himself."* The operator distinguishes; the implementation verifies.
